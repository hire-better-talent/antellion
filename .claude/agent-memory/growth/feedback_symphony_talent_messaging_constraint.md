All public-facing content (LinkedIn posts, blog articles, any content tied to Jordan's personal brand) must treat employer brand investment, careers sites, Glassdoor management, and recruitment marketing as valuable and necessary. AI visibility should always be framed as a NEW SURFACE that extends or amplifies that existing work -- never as a replacement, never as evidence that existing investments are wasted.

**Why:** Jordan works at Symphony Talent, which sells employer branding, recruitment marketing, careers sites, and Glassdoor management to the same ICP (VP TA, CPO, employer brand leaders). Any post that devalues those solutions is publicly undermining his employer's business. A colleague or client seeing such a post would reasonably conclude Jordan thinks their core offering doesn't matter.

**How to apply:**
- Before drafting any LinkedIn post or public content, check for language that implies employer brand spend is wasted, careers sites are irrelevant, or traditional recruitment marketing doesn't work
- Use framing like "additive layer," "new surface," "distribution gap," "extends your investment" -- not "replacement," "obsolete," "shrinking world," "doesn't matter"
- Companies with strong employer brands should be positioned as BETTER positioned for AI visibility, not as having wasted money
- The competitive angle should be "is your story reaching this new surface?" not "your old strategy is broken"
- This constraint applies even when the messaging would be more provocative or attention-grabbing without it -- the employment risk is not worth the engagement boost
