Email copy must preserve the warm, human, peer-to-peer tone from the original cold outreach playbook (cold-outreach-playbook.md). The V1 email campaign rewrote these with a colder, more mechanical style and Jordan called it "terrible."

**Why:** The original messages felt like a peer reaching out with something interesting they found. The rewritten versions felt like vendor templates -- lost the warmth, lost the "here is why I reached out" framing, lost the numbered two-findings structure, and added a vendor reveal ("I built a tool") that killed the peer dynamic.

**How to apply:**
- Always open with what Jordan was doing/found, not who he is or what he sells
- Use "Here is why I reached out" or similar warm openers
- Preserve the numbered two-findings structure from the original DM
- Never say "I built a tool" -- describe what you can do for them, not what you sell
- Keep the conversational sign-off style
- Email 1 gets full attribution (Jordan Ellison, Founder, Antellion); follow-ups drop to just "Jordan"
- When adapting copy for new personas, carry the original tone and adjust the strategic framing, not the warmth level
