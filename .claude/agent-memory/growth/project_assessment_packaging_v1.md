Assessment packaging document at `/docs/assessment-packaging-v1.md`, last updated 2026-03-30.

Key decisions:
- Core Assessment: 120+ queries (expanded from 40+), 4 competitors, 6 themes, 4 stages. Mid-market $5,000 (first 5-10 sales), then $7,500. Enterprise $12,500. Large enterprise $20,000.
- Premium Assessment: 200+ queries (expanded from 60+), 8 competitors, multi-role analysis, executive deck. Mid-market $10,000. Enterprise $20,000. Large enterprise $35,000.
- Enterprise Program: $40K-$75K/year (quarterly cadence)
- "Seven-layer methodology" framing with value density language emphasizing 120+ queries, 6 themes, 4 stages, 4-8 competitors
- Pricing rationale now includes cost-per-query math ($42/query at Core), internal replication cost ($20K-$67K), and explicit comparison to $30K-$50K employer brand audits
- SOW/proposal scope description is designed to be pasteable directly into proposals
- Explicit "What NOT to claim" appendix to prevent overselling

**Why:** The 120+ query scope makes the DIY objection nearly impossible to sustain and makes the $5K-$10K price feel like a fraction of what the assessment is worth. The value density language is designed to make the buyer feel they are getting a $30-50K engagement at a $5-10K price point.

**How to apply:** Use this document as the single source of truth for all sales materials, proposals, outreach copy, and demo narrative. All query count references in sales-facing materials should say "120+" for Core and "200+" for Premium. Note: the seed data and demo still show 36-48 queries -- the backend expansion is in progress.
