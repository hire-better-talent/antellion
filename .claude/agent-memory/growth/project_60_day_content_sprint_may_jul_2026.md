The full plan lives at `docs/60-day-content-sprint-may-jul-2026.md` (authored 2026-05-11). This memory captures the strategic shifts and constraints that will outlast the sprint specifics.

**The 60-day differentiation thesis:** Symphony Talent is productizing AI visibility audits using Profound (generic brand-side tool). Window to make hiring-specific differentiation visible and undeniable is open NOW and closes when ST publishes a formal service-line page or case study. Sprint content must establish that brand-side AI visibility and hiring-specific AI visibility are not the same product, without ever critiquing brand-side work or naming ST/Profound.

**Why:** ST is Jordan's day-job employer. Anti-employer-brand framing is both a positioning error (RULE-01) and a personal conflict of interest. ST going to market validates the category but doesn't compete on the differentiated layer if Antellion claims the methodology depth, persona-coding, and hiring-specific framing before ST locks in productized positioning.

**How to apply:**

1. **Contrarian template is removed for the full 60-day window.** Includes Tier 1 high-performing patterns from the April sprint: "your SEO agency cannot do this," "your audit doesn't measure this," "your current tools cannot see this." These now read as anti-ST. Templates available: Data Point, Framework, Question, How-to. Can return after Month 3 review or after Month 6.

2. **The ST additive test must pass for every LinkedIn post:** (a) does the hook imply current audit/brand work is inadequate? (b) does the post compare to a vendor or vendor category, even abstractly? (c) does it land on insight or critique? (d) would it have made sense to publish in February 2026? Run all four before publishing.

3. **Capstone benchmark is the anchor piece.** "State of AI Employer Visibility — H1 2026" publishes Tuesday June 30. Triple purpose: meta-GEO citation magnet for H2 2026, every Snapshot delivery gets a one-page Findings Brief, ST cannot publish a comparable benchmark without committing to hiring-specific methodology. Founder dependency: scan-corpus must support 150-250 scored responses minimum across 8-15 companies with sector mix.

4. **Tier 1 expansion (CRO/CTO/VP CS) does not get a content track yet.** Persona-depth posts (Senior AE, Staff Engineer, Senior CSM) use those personas as worked examples *for CHRO buyers*, not as Tier 1 buyer pitches. Per `docs/expansion-buyer-map.md`, Tier 1 expansion is Months 4–6 and gates on funnel-validation completion. Posts must close off "Antellion is HR-only" misread without pitching the non-HR reader.

5. **Short-form video is killed for the full 60 days.** April plan deferred conditionally; this plan retires it. Revealed preference (zero videos shipped in April), audience reach math (primary buyers not on TikTok/Reels in pro mode), and capstone-time competition all confirm. Revisit at Month 3 review July 10.

6. **Mid-sprint review June 10.** Hold-or-go decision points. Specific pivot triggers for ST escalation (service-line page, case study, sales-team conflict): 72-hour reaction window for first two; immediate founder-decision for sales conflict.

7. **Recommended RULE-01 tightening (NOT yet applied):** add pattern hints for comparative-without-replacement framings — "your audit doesn't measure this," "most audits miss this layer," "the standard approach has a gap." Surfaced as recommendation in §4 of the plan; founder edits `docs/compliance-rules.md` directly when ready.

8. **Cadence: 2 blog posts/week, 3 LinkedIn posts/week. 14 new blog posts + ~27 LinkedIn posts over the 9 weeks.** Sprint-end library: 24 blog posts. Estimated founder writing budget: ~60 hours over 60 days.

9. **The biggest open question is capstone data sufficiency.** If scan corpus is short of floor as of May 11, founder needs to allocate scan-running time in Weeks 1–4 of the sprint to backfill. Capstone success is the highest-leverage outcome.
