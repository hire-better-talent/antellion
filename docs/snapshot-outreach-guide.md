# Snapshot Outreach & Positioning Guide

**Purpose:** Specific copy, subject lines, talk tracks, and follow-up sequences for using the AI Employer Visibility Snapshot to drive paid Assessment conversions. Not a strategy deck. Everything here is usable tomorrow.

**Date:** March 2026

---

## 1. Snapshot Positioning

### What it is (one sentence)

A data-backed, 1-page analysis showing a company's AI mention rate, top competitor gap, and citation blind spots — produced from real candidate-intent queries, not a generic template.

### How to describe it to prospects

Never call it a "free report." The word "report" sounds like a PDF lead magnet. The word "free" devalues it before they open it.

Use these framings depending on context:

- **In outreach:** "Your AI Employer Visibility Snapshot — your mention rate, top competitor gap, and the 3 citation sources working for them but not you."
- **On a call:** "We ran your company through 100 candidate-intent AI queries and scored the results. Here is what we found."
- **On the website:** "Get your Visibility Snapshot — see how AI describes your company to candidates."
- **Internal shorthand:** "The Snapshot" (always capitalized when referring to this specific deliverable).

The Snapshot should feel like something a smart analyst prepared specifically for them. Not something a tool generated for anyone who fills out a form.

### Psychological trigger

Three triggers working together:

1. **Data specificity.** The Snapshot names their company, names their competitor, and shows a real number. "47% — Moderate" is not a concept. It is their score. People engage with data about themselves.

2. **Competitive anxiety.** The top competitor comparison is the sharpest element. "Apex Cloud is 1.6x more visible to candidates using AI" creates an immediate emotional response that no amount of educational content can match. They do not need to understand the category to feel the gap.

3. **Incomplete picture.** The Snapshot shows the score but not the exam. It answers "how visible are you?" but not "which candidate questions are you losing?" or "what should you do first?" This is the curiosity gap that makes the Assessment feel necessary rather than nice-to-have.

### How it differs from what agencies or competitors offer for free

| What others offer for free | What the Snapshot does differently |
|---|---|
| Glassdoor/Comparably ratings summary | Shows AI-synthesized perception, not review-site scores. Different data surface entirely. |
| "Employer brand health check" PDFs | Uses real AI responses to real candidate queries, not a self-assessment questionnaire or generic benchmarks. |
| SEO visibility audits | Measures AI answer surfaces, not search rankings. AI does not rank pages — it synthesizes answers. |
| Consultant "preliminary assessment" | Delivers actual numbers (mention rate, competitor gap, citation sources) instead of a teaser conversation leading to a proposal. |

The Snapshot gives away real, specific data. This is what makes it work. A prospect who receives their Snapshot has learned something they did not know before. They feel they got value. And they also feel the pull of everything it does not cover.

### Usage notes for all three templates

- **Personalize the data point.** Every template has bracketed placeholders for real numbers. These must come from an actual quick scan of the prospect's company. Generic outreach ("AI is changing how candidates evaluate employers!") will not work. The data is the hook.
- **Do not attach the Snapshot to the first email.** The first email offers to create it. This establishes that it is custom work, not a mass download.
- **Keep it under 150 words in the body.** Enterprise buyers scan. The email's job is to get one reaction: "That is interesting. Yes, send it."
- **Do not mention pricing, product names beyond Antellion, or the Assessment in the first email.** The CTA is the Snapshot, not a demo or a sales call.

## 3. What to Show vs. Withhold

### What the Snapshot reveals (and why it creates urgency, not satisfaction)

| Element revealed | Why revealing it drives urgency |
|---|---|
| **AI mention rate with tier (e.g., "47% — Moderate")** | A score invites comparison. "Moderate" is not reassuring — it says "you are not where you should be" without being alarming enough to dismiss. The prospect immediately wonders: what does "strong" look like? What does it take to get there? |
| **Sentiment label (Positive / Neutral / Negative)** | A single word that validates ("Positive — good, but am I visible enough?") or alarms ("Neutral — what is AI saying that is dragging this down?"). Either way, it raises a question the Snapshot does not answer: which queries are producing negative or neutral responses? |
| **Top competitor name, mention rate, gap, and visibility multiple** | This is the sharpest element. Seeing a named competitor beat you by 28 percentage points triggers competitive instinct. But the Snapshot shows only the top competitor. The prospect wonders: how do all my competitors compare? Who else is ahead? |
| **3 citation gap domains with source type and action** | Naming specific platforms (levels.fyi, builtin.com) makes the gap tangible and immediately actionable. But showing only 3 out of a potentially longer list signals there is more. And the recommended actions are brief — "Claim or update profile" — not a remediation plan. |
| **4-sentence summary ending with Assessment upsell** | The summary reinforces the data and ends with a clear statement of what the full Assessment would cover. This is the explicit bridge. |

**The key design principle:** Every element in the Snapshot gives the prospect something they did not know before, but simultaneously raises a question they cannot answer with the Snapshot alone.

### What the Snapshot withholds (and why each withheld element drives Assessment conversion)

| Element withheld | Why withholding it drives conversion |
|---|---|
| **Per-theme breakdown (which candidate questions they win/lose)** | The Snapshot shows the aggregate score. But a VP of TA who sees "47% mention rate" immediately wants to know: is that 47% spread evenly, or are we strong on some themes and invisible on others? The theme breakdown is the "aha moment" in the full Assessment — it reveals that a company might be 83% visible on hiring process queries but 17% visible on compensation queries. That specificity is what makes the Assessment feel indispensable. |
| **Full competitor profiles (all competitors, not just the top one)** | The Snapshot names one competitor. The prospect has 3-5 they care about. "How do we compare against all of them?" is a question only the Assessment answers. |
| **Prioritized recommendations with rationale and action plans** | The Snapshot shows citation gaps with one-line actions. The Assessment delivers a prioritized remediation plan — what to fix first, why, how, and what timeline to expect. The gap between "you should claim your Built In profile" and "here are the 5 highest-impact actions in priority order with expected visibility lift" is the value gap that justifies the Assessment price. |
| **Methodology and scope section** | Enterprise buyers want to understand how the data was produced. The Assessment includes methodology, scope, and query selection rationale. This is what makes it presentable to leadership — a Snapshot is a data point, an Assessment is a document you can hand to your CPO. |
| **Board-ready formatting and executive narrative** | The Snapshot is a 1-pager. The Assessment is a structured document with cover page, executive summary, competitive analysis, citation patterns, and recommendations — formatted for board presentation. The format difference is part of the value. |

### The curiosity gap

The Snapshot answers: **"How visible am I in AI?"**

The Snapshot raises but does not answer: **"Where am I losing, and what should I do about it?"**

This is the specific question that only the Assessment answers. The Snapshot gives a score and a gap. The Assessment gives the diagnosis and the plan. The prospect who receives the Snapshot understands their position but cannot act on it with confidence — and that is what creates the conversion pressure.

## 4. CTA Design

### CTA on the Snapshot itself

The Snapshot page already includes a footer upsell. The exact language in the summary block:

> "A full AI Employer Visibility Assessment would evaluate [Company] across 200-600 queries, analyze all competitors, and produce a prioritized remediation plan."

This is the right framing — it is specific about what the Assessment adds (more queries, all competitors, prioritized plan) without sounding like a sales pitch. It reads as an informational note, which is appropriate for a document the prospect may forward internally.

**Do not add a "Buy Now" or "Schedule a Demo" button to the Snapshot.** The Snapshot is a standalone deliverable, not a landing page. The sales motion happens in the follow-up, not on the document.

### CTA in the outreach email

First email (before the Snapshot exists):
> "Want me to run it?"

This works because it is low-friction, conversational, and implies custom work. It is not "schedule a call" or "request a demo." It is "yes" or "no."

Follow-up email (after the Snapshot is delivered):
> "Three things stood out in your results. Worth a 20-minute walkthrough?"

This works because it references specific findings (not generic) and asks for a small time commitment (20 minutes, not "a call").

### Verbal CTA in a live walkthrough

When walking through the Snapshot on a call:

> "This is what we can see from a 100-query scan. The full Assessment runs 200 to 600 queries, covers all [number] of your competitors, breaks results down by theme — compensation, culture, hiring process — and produces a prioritized remediation plan. It takes about two to three weeks. Want me to scope it for your team?"

Key phrases:
- "What we can see from a 100-query scan" — frames the Snapshot as a starting point, not the product.
- "Breaks results down by theme" — this is the hook. Theme-level data is where the insight lives, and the prospect cannot get it from the Snapshot.
- "Want me to scope it" — positions the next step as proposal, not purchase. Enterprise buyers respond to "let me show you what this would look like for you" not "here is the price."

### Transition framing (Snapshot to Assessment)

The transition should feel like a natural escalation of the analysis, not a product sale. Framing that works for enterprise:

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---|---|
| "Want to upgrade to the full product?" | "Want us to run the full analysis?" |
| "The Snapshot was the free version..." | "The Snapshot covers 100 queries. The full Assessment covers 200-600 across all your competitors." |
| "Our paid Assessment includes..." | "The Assessment would give your team the theme-level breakdown and a prioritized remediation plan." |
| "Ready to buy?" | "Worth scoping this for your team?" |

The language should always frame the Assessment as more analysis, not more product.

## 5. Objection Handling

### "We already have an employer brand strategy."

> "That is exactly why this matters. You have invested in employer brand — EVP, careers content, culture storytelling. The question is whether AI is using any of it. When candidates ask ChatGPT about employers in your space, the AI synthesizes hundreds of sources into a single answer. If the right sources are not in the mix, your employer brand investment may not be reaching the fastest-growing candidate research channel. The Assessment tells you whether it is."

**What this does:** Validates their investment. Does not challenge their strategy. Introduces AI as a channel their strategy may not yet cover. Positions the Assessment as a way to protect their existing investment, not replace it.

---

### "We use Glassdoor/Comparably already."

> "Glassdoor is valuable — it tells you what employees say about you. Antellion shows you something different: what AI says about you. And AI does not just read Glassdoor. It synthesizes Glassdoor, Blind, Built In, your careers page, press coverage, technical blogs, and dozens of other sources into a single answer. The candidate who asks ChatGPT 'where should I work in fintech?' gets a synthesized recommendation — not a link to Glassdoor. We measure how you show up in that answer."

**What this does:** Avoids dismissing Glassdoor (the prospect likely pays for it). Positions Antellion as complementary, not competitive. Reframes the question from "what do employees say?" to "what does AI say?" — a different surface entirely.

---

### "AI isn't how candidates find us."

> "You are probably right that most of your hires today come through job boards, referrals, and direct applications. But candidate research behavior is shifting. Internal Microsoft data showed Bing AI queries growing over 50% year-over-year. Gartner projects that by 2028, a significant share of information-seeking queries will go through AI. The question is not whether your current candidates use AI — it is whether the candidates you are losing are using it, and what they are hearing. The Snapshot takes 48 hours and costs you nothing. At minimum, you will know where you stand."

**What this does:** Does not argue with the prospect or tell them they are wrong. Reframes from "how candidates find us today" to "what the candidates we never see are hearing." The Snapshot offer at the end makes it low-risk to engage.

---

### "What's different about this vs. SEO?"

> "SEO optimizes for search rankings — getting your page to appear higher in Google results. AI works differently. AI models do not rank pages. They read hundreds of sources, synthesize them, and generate a single answer. There is no 'page 1 of results' in AI. There is one answer, and your company is either in it or it is not. The signals that influence AI answers — which citation sources mention you, how consistently your employer narrative appears across platforms, what sentiment patterns exist — are different from the signals that drive search rankings. Antellion measures the AI surface specifically."

**What this does:** Gives a clear, concrete explanation of the difference. Does not dismiss SEO. Establishes that AI visibility is a distinct problem that requires distinct measurement.

---

### "Can we just fix the gaps ourselves?"

> "Honestly — yes, some of them. If the Snapshot shows you are missing a Built In profile or a Levels.fyi listing, your team can go create those this week. That is the point of showing citation gaps. But the harder questions are: which gaps matter most? What is the priority order? Are there theme-level blind spots — like being invisible on compensation queries but strong on hiring process? And how do you measure whether the fixes actually moved your AI visibility? The Assessment provides the diagnosis across 200-600 queries, the competitive comparison across all your competitors, the theme-level breakdown, and a prioritized remediation plan. Your team absolutely can execute the fixes. The Assessment tells them which fixes to execute first and how to measure progress."

**What this does:** Validates their instinct to act. Does not gatekeep. Positions the Assessment as the diagnostic layer — not the only way to fix things, but the best way to know what to fix and in what order. The prospect should feel that Antellion respects their capability while adding strategic value.

### Sequence summary

| Timing | Purpose | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 | Deliver the Snapshot with highlights | Informational, generous |
| Day 3 | Surface one specific insight, offer walkthrough | Analytical, helpful |
| Day 7 | Introduce competitive angle, name the Assessment scope | Consultative, data-led |
| Day 14 | Direct Assessment offer with scope, guarantee, timeline | Clear, professional |
| Day 21+ | Close the loop, leave the door open | Respectful, no pressure |

### When to escalate and when to walk away

- **Escalate from "want to discuss?" to Assessment scope** at Day 7 if the prospect opened the Snapshot email but has not replied. The Day 7 email introduces what the Assessment includes without asking for a commitment.
- **Make the direct offer** at Day 14 regardless of engagement. By this point, the prospect has had the Snapshot for two weeks. If the data was compelling, they have had time to think about it. If not, the direct offer either moves them forward or confirms they are not in-market.
- **Walk away** at Day 21 if there is no engagement. Do not send more than 4 follow-ups total (delivery + 3 follow-ups). Enterprise buyers respect restraint. Persistent follow-up past this point damages credibility.
- **Exception:** If the prospect opened every email but never replied, a Day 28 LinkedIn connection request with a one-sentence note ("Sent you a Visibility Snapshot for [Company] a few weeks back — thought it might be useful") is acceptable. One touchpoint, no pitch.

## Quick Reference: Language Rules

**Always say:**
- "Visibility Snapshot" (capitalized, two words)
- "AI Employer Visibility Assessment" (the paid product)
- "mention rate" (not "visibility score" — the Snapshot shows mention rate)
- "citation gaps" (not "missing sources" or "link gaps")
- "candidate-intent queries" (not "search queries" or "prompts")

**Never say:**
- "Free report" (diminishes perceived value)
- "Limited time" or "act now" (enterprise buyers recoil)
- "AI is disrupting..." (commodity hype)
- "Transform your talent strategy" (meaningless)
- "Upgrade to the full version" (sounds like SaaS upsell, not strategic analysis)

**Never claim:**
- That Antellion improves AI responses directly (we measure and recommend; the client acts)
- Real-time monitoring (scans are point-in-time)
- Precise rankings (structured assessments, not rankings)
- Guaranteed timelines for visibility improvement (recommend 90-day re-assessment cycles)
