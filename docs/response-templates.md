# Response Templates — Cold Email Reply Handling

Paste these when a prospect replies to the CTO V1 cold email sequence in Instantly. Merge fields use Instantly's `{{firstName}}` / `{{companyName}}` syntax — Instantly auto-substitutes when you reply from the campaign thread.

**Response time goal:** Under 2 hours during business hours. Speed signals a real person is sending, not an SDR tool.

**Rule:** Never copy a template verbatim without reading their reply. Adjust one line to reference what they actually said. The template is scaffolding, not output.

---

## Group A: Positive replies

### 1. Interested — Info Collection

Use when the prospect says "interested," "tell me more," "sounds useful," or asks a qualifying question about the Snapshot itself.

```
{{firstName}} —

Great. Here is what I need to run your Snapshot:

1. Company domain (confirming: {{companyName}}.com — update if wrong)
2. Your biggest talent competitor (who do you lose candidates to most?)
3. Primary roles you hire for (e.g., engineers, sales, product)

You can also submit this at antellion.com if that is easier.

I will have your results within 48 hours of receiving the above.

Jordan
```

### 2. Interested — Form Redirect

Use when the prospect seems warm but conversational. Directs them to self-serve the form.

```
{{firstName}} —

Glad this resonated. The fastest path: submit your company info at antellion.com — takes 60 seconds, and I will have your Snapshot back within 48 hours.

If you have a specific competitor you want compared, include that in the form. If not, I will identify your most visible competitor from the scan data.

Jordan
```

## Group B: Not-yet replies

### 3. Bad Timing / Not Right Now

```
{{firstName}} —

Completely understand. I will check back in a quarter with updated observations about how AI describes {{companyName}} to candidates. If anything changes on your end before then, the offer stands.

Jordan
```

**Then:** Add to 90-day follow-up list in the tracker. When you follow up, lead with a new finding, not a "just checking in."

## Group C: Objection handlers

### 4. "We already manage our employer brand."

```
{{firstName}} —

That is exactly why the Snapshot is worth seeing. It measures whether your employer brand investment is reaching the AI channel — the one surface most talent teams have not mapped yet. Companies with strong employer brands are often the most surprised by where they stand in AI, not because the brand work is wrong but because AI draws from a different set of sources than candidates used to use.

The Snapshot takes you 60 seconds to request and 48 hours to receive. No call required to see it. If the data is useful, we talk. If not, you have it.

Jordan
```

### 5. "Can I just ask ChatGPT myself?"

```
{{firstName}} —

You can, and I recommend trying a few queries to build intuition. What you cannot replicate with a handful of questions is 100 queries across the four stages of the candidate decision journey, consistent scoring, ranked competitor comparison, and a source-by-source citation analysis.

The volume and structure are what turn a curiosity into a measurement you can use in a board conversation. That is the difference.

Jordan
```

```
{{firstName}} —

Happy to send a one-page privacy summary if useful for internal review.

Jordan
```

### 7. "What qualifies you to do this?"

```

Fair question. I have spent the last year studying how AI models represent employers — which sources they cite, where the gaps are, and how the signal differs from traditional employer brand channels. I built the methodology because no one else was measuring this specifically for the candidate-intent use case.

The Snapshot output will answer most of that. Happy to walk through the approach on a call once you have seen your results.

Jordan

## Group D: Edge cases

### 8. Wrong Person / Routed Elsewhere

Use when the prospect says "this should go to [other name]" or "I'm not the right person."

```

Thanks for pointing me in the right direction. Is it OK if I reach out to {{redirectedContactName}} directly and reference this thread, or would you prefer to forward it?

Either way, appreciate the time.

Jordan
```

Use when the prospect asks about cost — usually after seeing the Snapshot and inquiring about the full assessment.

```

The Full Assessment sits at $5K-$15K depending on scope (number of competitors, depth of remediation recommendations, executive presentation). I can walk through what is included once you have seen your Snapshot and know whether the direction is useful.

Jordan
