# LinkedIn Cold Outreach Playbook

**Purpose:** Ready-to-send LinkedIn DM copy for cold outreach to talent leaders, with psychological reasoning, personalization system, and operational guidance. Every message below is designed to get one reaction: "That is interesting. Yes, send it."

**Date:** April 2026
**Sender:** Jordan Ellison, Founder, Antellion
**Audience:** VP Talent Acquisition, Head of Employer Brand, VP/CPO at companies with 200+ employees
**Offer:** AI Employer Visibility Snapshot -- 100 candidate-intent queries across AI, delivered in 48 hours, no cost

---

## The Strategic Frame

Before the copy: understand why this works differently from every other vendor DM in their inbox.

The Snapshot is not a lead magnet in the traditional sense. It is a **diagnostic that reveals a problem the prospect does not know they have**. This is the most powerful type of lead magnet (Hormozi's "Reveal Their Problem" archetype). It works because:

1. **They cannot get this data anywhere else.** No one else scans AI for employer visibility. There is nothing to compare it to. This makes the offer incomparable -- you are not competing with other vendors, you are offering something that does not exist in their world yet.

2. **The value equation is massively in their favor.** Dream Outcome (know what AI tells candidates about your company) is high. Perceived Likelihood (we run it and send results -- they do nothing) is high. Time Delay (48 hours) is low. Effort (reply "interested" or fill out a form) is near zero. The math makes it irrational to say no.

3. **The curiosity gap is irresistible.** Every talent leader manages Glassdoor, careers pages, and LinkedIn. None of them have ever seen what AI tells candidates about their company. The moment they think "I actually don't know," the message has done its job.

4. **It feels like a favor, not a pitch.** The best cold outreach creates the sensation of receiving a gift from a peer, not a proposal from a vendor. Everything below is written to preserve that feeling.

## Message 1: Connection Request (~300 characters)

### The Message

> I've been researching how AI describes employers in [INDUSTRY] to candidates. [COMPANY] came up in an interesting way. Happy to share what I found if useful -- no pitch, just data.

**Character count:** ~195 (leaves room for industry/company name variation)

### Why It Works

- **Opens with what you were doing, not what you want.** "I've been researching" positions you as someone doing analytical work, not someone prospecting a list. It implies you have already done something involving their company.
- **"Came up in an interesting way" is a curiosity gap.** It is specific enough to feel real and vague enough that they need to accept the connection to find out what you mean. This is not clickbait -- you will actually have something to share. But the connection request is not the place to share it.
- **"No pitch, just data" is a risk reversal.** It directly addresses the reason they ignore 90% of connection requests. It works because you mean it. The follow-up DM delivers data, not a sales deck.
- **No ask.** The connection request does not ask them to do anything except accept. The CTA is implicit: accept the connection and I will share what I found.

### Personalization Variables

| Variable | Where to find it | Example |
|----------|-----------------|---------|
| [INDUSTRY] | Their LinkedIn headline or company page | "enterprise SaaS," "healthcare tech," "financial services" |
| [COMPANY] | Their current employer | "Datadog," "HCA Healthcare," "Stripe" |

### Variant for Employer Brand Leaders

> Curious whether your EVP work at [COMPANY] is reaching AI. I ran some candidate-intent queries and found a gap worth flagging. Happy to share if useful.

**Why the variant works:** Employer brand leaders have invested heavily in EVP. Suggesting that investment might not be reaching an entire channel (AI) creates a very specific, role-relevant anxiety. "A gap worth flagging" is specific without being alarming.

## Message 2: Follow-Up DM (After Connection Accepted, No Response)

Send 24-48 hours after they accept but do not respond. The accept without reply means they were curious enough to connect but not motivated enough to initiate. This message provides the motivation.

### The Message

> Thanks for connecting. Here is what I mentioned --

> I ran a set of candidate-intent queries through AI this week (the kinds of questions engineers, PMs, and analysts ask ChatGPT before deciding where to apply). I included [COMPANY] in the scan.

> Two things stood out:

> 1. [COMPETITOR] appeared in significantly more AI responses than [COMPANY] for queries in your space.
> 2. There are at least 2 citation sources AI uses to recommend [COMPETITOR] where [COMPANY] has no presence.

> This is not a Glassdoor or SEO issue -- AI synthesizes hundreds of sources into one answer, and right now that answer favors your competitors more often than it favors you.

> I can put together a full Visibility Snapshot -- your AI mention rate, competitor gap, and the specific citation blind spots -- in 48 hours. No cost, no call needed.

> Worth running?

### Why It Works

- **Delivers on the promise from the connection request.** You said you would share what you found. This message does exactly that. It builds trust immediately.
- **Two specific findings, not a generic pitch.** The numbered list gives them real data about their company. This is not "AI is changing hiring!" -- it is "[Competitor] appears more than you do" and "here are citation gaps." Specificity is credibility.
- **Competitive anxiety is the sharpest motivator.** Naming a specific competitor creates an emotional reaction that no amount of educational content can match. They do not need to understand the category to feel the gap.
- **"No cost, no call needed" removes all friction.** Hormozi's value equation: maximize Dream Outcome and Perceived Likelihood, minimize Time Delay and Effort. This sentence crushes the denominator -- there is no time cost (no call) and no financial cost. The only thing they invest is a reply.
- **"Worth running?" is the lowest-friction CTA possible.** It is one word away from "yes." It is conversational, not transactional. It positions you as someone offering to do work for them, not someone asking for their time.

### Personalization Variables

| Variable | Where to find it | How to research |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| [COMPANY] | Their profile | -- |
| [COMPETITOR] | Industry knowledge, job postings, press | Ask: "who does [COMPANY] compete with for talent?" Look at their job postings, press mentions, or ask ChatGPT directly |
| Role types | Their company's open positions | Check their careers page for common roles |

### Important: The Data Must Be Real

Do not send this message with fabricated findings. Before sending, run 5-10 quick queries in ChatGPT or Claude:

- "Best [industry] companies to work for"
- "Should I work at [Company] or [Competitor]?"
- "What is it like to work at [Company]?"
- "[Company] vs [Competitor] for engineers"

Scan the responses. Note whether the company appears, whether the competitor appears, and which sources AI cites. Use those real observations in the message. If the company actually performs well, adjust the message to reflect that -- "your visibility is solid but there are still gaps in X." Authenticity is the only thing that separates this from the vendor spam they delete.

## Message 3: InMail Version (For Prospects You Cannot Connect With Directly)

### Subject Line

> What AI tells candidates about [COMPANY]

**Why it works:** It is a direct statement that raises an immediate question: "What does AI say about us?" The subject line does not sell. It does not ask for time. It names their company and introduces a concept they have never considered. Most InMail subject lines are about the sender ("I'd love to connect," "Partnership opportunity"). This one is about the recipient.

### Subject Line Variants (test these)

- **For VP TA:** [COMPANY] vs. [COMPETITOR] -- in AI candidate queries
- **For Employer Brand:** Your EVP might not be reaching AI
- **For CPO/CHRO:** A question for your next board talent update

### The Message

> [First name] --

> I've been analyzing how AI describes employers to candidates -- the answers people get when they ask ChatGPT or Claude "best companies to work for in [industry]" or "should I join [Company] or [Competitor]?"

> I included [COMPANY] in a scan this week. Two observations:

> Your top competitor for talent, [COMPETITOR], appeared in noticeably more AI responses than [COMPANY] across the queries I tested. And there are citation sources -- platforms AI relies on when recommending employers in your space -- where [COMPETITOR] has a presence and [COMPANY] does not.

> This does not show up in your ATS data or your Glassdoor dashboard. It is a different surface entirely. AI does not rank pages -- it synthesizes hundreds of signals into a single answer. Right now, that answer is more likely to mention [COMPETITOR] than [COMPANY].

> I can put together your AI Employer Visibility Snapshot: your mention rate, competitor gap, and the specific citation blind spots creating the gap. It covers 100 candidate-intent queries and takes 48 hours. No cost, no commitment, and no call needed -- I will send it directly to your inbox.

> If you want me to run it, just reply "interested" or submit your info at antellion.com. Either way takes 30 seconds.

> -- Jordan Ellison
> Founder, Antellion

### Why It Works

- **Opens with what you do, not who you are.** "I've been analyzing" establishes expertise through activity, not credentials. It positions the sender as a researcher, not a salesperson.
- **Names the AI models.** "ChatGPT or Claude" is specific. "AI tools" is vague. Specificity is the single biggest trust signal in enterprise outreach.
- **Two findings structure.** Same as the DM version -- gives them real, specific data. Not three findings (too much for a first message), not one (too thin). Two is the right number: enough to be credible, not so much that they feel they already got the answer.
- **"This does not show up in your ATS data or your Glassdoor dashboard" is the reframe.** It tells them this is not something they have already solved. It is not a Glassdoor problem or an SEO problem. It is a new surface they have never measured. This is what Hormozi calls "selling in a vacuum" -- when the prospect cannot compare your offer to anything else, you are in a category of one.
- **Two response paths, both near-zero effort.** Reply "interested" (one word) or visit the site (60 seconds). The dual CTA means they pick whichever feels easiest in the moment. Both convert.
- **No meeting, no demo, no call.** This is the nuclear differentiation. Every other vendor in their inbox asks for 30 minutes. You are asking for a one-word reply and giving them 48 hours of analytical work in return. The asymmetry is what makes it feel like a gift.

## Message 4: The Re-Engage (If They Viewed Your Profile But Did Not Respond)

LinkedIn shows you when someone views your profile. If a prospect views your profile after receiving your connection request or InMail but does not respond, send this within 48 hours.

### The Message

> Noticed you checked out my profile -- if you are curious about what AI says about [COMPANY] to candidates, I am happy to run the scan. Takes 48 hours, nothing needed from your end.

> No pressure either way.

**Character count:** ~230

### Why It Works

- **Acknowledges their behavior without being creepy.** "Noticed you checked out my profile" is a normal LinkedIn interaction. It signals awareness without surveillance.
- **Reframes the same offer in fewer words.** They already saw the longer version. This is the simplified reminder. Sometimes the timing just was not right the first time.
- **"No pressure either way" is genuine.** It creates the opposite of sales pressure. Enterprise buyers are so conditioned to pressure that the absence of it is itself a differentiator.

## What NOT to Say

These are the patterns that get your message deleted, ignored, or flagged as spam. Every one of them is common in talent-tech vendor outreach. Avoid them completely.

### Do Not Open With Who You Are

Bad: "Hi [Name], I'm Jordan, founder of Antellion, an AI employer visibility platform..."

The prospect does not care who you are. They care about what you know about THEM. Lead with what you found, not your bio. Your name and company appear in your LinkedIn profile. They can look if they are curious.

### Do Not Use Hype Language

Bad: "AI is disrupting how candidates discover employers..."

This sounds like every other AI vendor pitch since 2023. It is category-level language that creates no urgency about their specific company. Replace category education with company-specific data.

### Do Not Ask For Their Time Before Giving Value

Bad: "Would you have 15 minutes this week to discuss how AI is changing employer perception?"

You have given them nothing. Why would they give you 15 minutes? The entire point of the Snapshot offer is to invert this: you give them value first (the Snapshot), and they give you time after (the walkthrough).

### Do Not Say "Free"

Bad: "I'd like to offer you a free AI employer visibility report..."

"Free report" sounds like a PDF lead magnet. It devalues the Snapshot before they see it. The Snapshot is custom analytical work. Frame it as "no cost" if you must reference price, but better to not mention cost at all -- just describe what they get and let the absence of a price signal generosity.

### Do Not Use Visible Merge Tags or Template Language

Bad: "Companies like {{company_name}} in {{industry}} are..."

Even if your tools are good, template-feeling language kills credibility. Write as if you wrote this message for one person, because functionally, you did.

### Do Not Claim Things the Product Cannot Do

Bad: "We can improve your AI visibility in 30 days..."

Antellion measures and recommends. It does not directly improve AI responses. Do not overclaim. Enterprise buyers are sophisticated enough to catch it, and it destroys the trust you are trying to build.

### Do Not Stack CTAs

Bad: "Would you like to schedule a call, or I could send you a report, or you could check out our website at..."

One CTA. "Want me to run it?" or "Reply interested." Decision fatigue kills conversion. Give them one thing to do.

## How to Personalize at Scale

The messages above are templates, but they should never feel like templates. Here is the system for personalizing each message in under 3 minutes.

### The 3-Minute Research Protocol

For each prospect, before sending:

**Step 1: Identify their competitor (30 seconds)**
- Check their LinkedIn headline and company page
- Ask yourself: "Who does this company lose candidates to?"
- If unsure, search "[Company] vs" in Google or ask ChatGPT "who competes with [Company] for engineering talent?"

**Step 2: Run 3-5 AI queries (90 seconds)**
- Open ChatGPT or Claude
- Ask: "Best [industry] companies to work for as a [common role at their company]"
- Ask: "Should I work at [Company] or [Competitor]?"
- Ask: "What is the culture like at [Company]?"
- Note: Does the company appear? Does the competitor? What sources does AI cite?

**Step 3: Identify one specific observation (30 seconds)**
- Pick the most interesting finding from the queries
- Frame it as a gap, a strength, or a surprise
- This becomes the hook in your message

**Step 4: Customize the message (30 seconds)**
- Swap in company name, competitor name, industry, and role types
- Adjust the "two things that stood out" to reflect your actual findings
- If the company performed well in AI, adjust accordingly: "Your visibility is actually solid in some areas, but there is a specific gap on [theme] that [Competitor] is winning"

### Batch Research Strategy

To make this sustainable at 20-30 messages per day:

1. **Pick 5 companies in the same industry per batch.** Run the same 5 queries for all of them. The competitor landscape overlaps, so you get efficiency.
2. **Keep a running document of findings.** After the first batch, you will start recognizing patterns ("AI consistently cites Built In for tech companies," "Healthcare companies rarely appear in compensation queries"). These patterns become your domain expertise.
3. **Re-use competitor data.** If you scan Company A vs. Competitor B, you now have data on Competitor B. If Competitor B is also a prospect, you already have findings.

## When to Send

LinkedIn DMs have different engagement patterns than email. Here is what works for VP-level talent leaders.

### Best Days

- **Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday** -- highest engagement
- **Monday** -- acceptable, but morning DMs compete with weekend catch-up
- **Friday** -- lower engagement, but "end of week scan" messages can work if framed as something they can review Monday

### Best Times

- **7:30-9:00 AM (prospect's local time)** -- before their first meeting, scrolling LinkedIn with coffee. This is the highest-engagement window.
- **12:00-1:00 PM** -- lunch break LinkedIn scroll
- **5:00-6:30 PM** -- end of day decompression. Less likely to respond immediately, but more likely to accept connections

### Avoid

- **Weekends** -- it signals automation or desperation
- **After 8 PM** -- same
- **During major HR conferences** -- they are not on LinkedIn, they are in sessions. Wait until the day after the conference ends (they will be processing what they learned and more receptive to new ideas)

### Response Window

If they reply, respond within 2 hours during business hours. Speed signals that a real person is on the other end, which reinforces the "this is not automated" feel.

## The Full Sequence: Timeline and Escalation

| Day | Action | Channel |
|-----|--------|---------|
| 0 | Send connection request with note | LinkedIn |
| 1-3 | If accepted, no response: send Follow-Up DM | LinkedIn DM |
| 3-5 | If not accepted: send InMail | LinkedIn InMail |
| 7 | If accepted + DM sent, no response: send Re-Engage if they viewed profile, otherwise wait | LinkedIn DM |
| 14 | If no response to any touchpoint: move to quarterly nurture list | -- |

### Rules

- **Maximum 3 touchpoints** before moving to nurture. Enterprise buyers respect restraint. Persistent follow-up past this point damages credibility and your ability to come back later.
- **Never send the same message twice.** Each touchpoint adds new value or a new angle.
- **If they respond with ANY engagement** (even "not right now"), treat them as warm. Add to CRM and follow up in 90 days with an updated observation about their AI visibility.
- **If they respond "interested"**, move immediately to the Snapshot intake process. Send them the landing page link or ask for their company name, domain, and top talent competitor directly in DM.

## Mapping Messages to Buyer Personas

Different titles respond to different angles. Use the right variant.

### VP Talent Acquisition

**Primary anxiety:** Pipeline quality, competitive win rates, cost-per-hire
**Opening angle:** Competitor visibility gap (they care about losing candidates to named competitors)
**Key phrase to include:** "candidates researching employers through AI are more likely to hear about [Competitor] before they see your job posting"
**Avoid:** Abstract brand language. They want pipeline impact, not perception metrics.

### Head of Employer Brand

**Primary anxiety:** Whether their EVP work is reaching the right channels, proving ROI on brand investments
**Opening angle:** EVP-to-AI gap (they have invested in employer brand -- is AI using it?)
**Key phrase to include:** "your EVP work may not be reaching the fastest-growing candidate research channel"
**Avoid:** Anything that sounds like you are criticizing their brand work. Position AI as a new channel their existing strategy has not yet covered.

### CPO / CHRO

**Primary anxiety:** Board-level questions, strategic blind spots, organizational risk
**Opening angle:** Board readiness (if someone on the board asks "how does AI describe us as an employer," do you have an answer?)
**Key phrase to include:** "a blind spot that did not exist two years ago"
**Avoid:** Tactical details. CPOs want strategic framing, not mention rates and citation sources.

### VP People / Head of People

**Primary anxiety:** Culture perception, retention signals, employer reputation holistically
**Opening angle:** Perception accuracy (AI may be describing your culture in ways that do not match reality)
**Key phrase to include:** "what AI synthesizes about your culture may not reflect what your team has built"
**Avoid:** Heavy competitive framing. People leaders are less motivated by competitive anxiety than by accuracy and authenticity of their employer narrative.

## Measuring What Works

### Track These Metrics

| Metric | Target | How to track |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Connection acceptance rate | 30-40% | LinkedIn shows pending/accepted |
| DM response rate (after acceptance) | 15-25% | Count replies / DMs sent |
| InMail response rate | 8-15% | LinkedIn InMail analytics |
| "Interested" conversion rate | 40-60% of responders | Count Snapshot requests / total responses |
| Time from first touch to Snapshot request | Under 7 days | CRM or spreadsheet |

### What to Optimize First

1. **Connection acceptance rate below 25%:** Your connection note does not create enough curiosity. Test a different opening line. The most common failure is sounding like a vendor in the connection note.
2. **Acceptance rate good, DM response rate below 10%:** Your follow-up DM is not specific enough. The data you are citing does not feel real or relevant. Run better pre-send research.
3. **Response rate good, conversion to Snapshot request below 30%:** Your CTA has too much friction. Simplify. Remove any mention of calls, demos, or commitments.

## Language Rules (from Snapshot Outreach Guide)

Maintain these consistently across all messages.

**Always say:**
- "Visibility Snapshot" (capitalized, two words)
- "mention rate" (the primary metric)
- "citation gaps" or "citation blind spots"
- "candidate-intent queries"

**Never say:**
- "Free report" (diminishes value)
- "Limited time" or "act now" (enterprise buyers recoil)
- "AI is disrupting..." (commodity hype)
- "Transform your talent strategy" (meaningless)
- "Schedule a demo" (the Snapshot is not a demo)
- "I'd love to" (every vendor says this -- it is noise)

**Never claim:**
- That Antellion improves AI responses directly (we measure and recommend)
- Real-time monitoring (scans are point-in-time)
- Multi-model simultaneous scanning as a current capability
- Guaranteed timelines for visibility improvement
