## Post #18 — Tuesday, May 20

**Source:** Blog Post 14 (40-Query Coverage Floor)
**Template:** Framework
**Hormozi optimization:** Hook = specific math; Retain = named four-stage structure with one sentence per stage; Reward = the named "10-per-stage floor" as a memorable label the reader keeps.
**Compliance pass:** RULE-01 (positions around measurement structure, not against the adequacy of other scans), RULE-02, RULE-10 (sector-neutral — revenue and operations both surfaced), RULE-11, RULE-20, RULE-21, RULE-22

---

The number of queries in an AI employer visibility scan matters less than how the queries are distributed.

Most of the actionable findings in an AI visibility measurement live in the variance across candidate-journey stages, not in the average across queries. A candidate forms different impressions of the same company at different stages of their decision:

Discovery — "who builds customer success software in healthcare." Category-shaped. Dominated by trade publications, vertical newsletters, consolidated rating platforms.

Consideration — "is Company A or Company B better for a senior revenue leader." Comparative. Dominated by competitor co-mention patterns and rating-platform comparisons.

Evaluation — "what is the compensation structure at Company A." Specifics. Dominated by specialist surfaces and named-leader narratives.

Commitment — "how should I prepare for an interview at Company A." Tactical. Dominated by Glassdoor interview reports, Reddit threads, named-employee posts.

The same company can be described one way at Discovery and a materially different way at Evaluation, and the difference is the finding.

Ten queries per stage is the floor at which the difference becomes visible. Fewer than ten and the within-stage citation pattern is not yet observable. Forty queries — ten per stage across four stages — is the unit of work where the asymmetries surface reliably.

Above forty, additional queries return additional resolution at a declining rate. Below forty, the findings that drive candidate behavior are present in the response surface but not yet visible to the analyst.

The coverage floor is a structural fact about the candidate-journey, not a methodology preference.

## Post #19 — Thursday, May 22

**Source:** Blog Post 15 (Staff Engineer journey)
**Template:** Data Point
**Hormozi optimization:** Hook = the most jarring specific quote ("modern web technologies"); Retain = the consequence to a real candidate decision; Reward = the named pattern (model-pair divergence on stack representation) that the reader can apply.
**Compliance pass:** RULE-01, RULE-02, RULE-10 (engineering-specific exception — topic is genuinely tech), RULE-11 (no acronyms — "senior individual contributor" spelled out), RULE-20, RULE-21, RULE-22

---

When AI describes engineering at a healthcare-technology company, three of the four leading models surfaced the company's specific stack accurately — primary language, core frameworks, data infrastructure, deployment platform, all cited to the company's own engineering blog and two recent conference talks.

The fourth model described the same engineering organization as building on "modern web technologies."

Accurate, in the broadest sense. Useless, in the sense of helping a senior individual contributor decide whether the engineering depth at the company matches what they would be looking for.

The candidate consulting the fourth model walked away with a meaningfully thinner picture of the engineering organization than the candidate consulting the other three. Same company. Same question. Same week. Different AI.

For a chief HR officer and a chief technology officer trying to understand where the engineering pipeline is leaking, this is the kind of variance that produces invisible cost. The candidate who saw the thin answer does not become an applicant. The candidate who saw the dense answer does. The recruiter sees the second outcome and the company never knows the first one happened.

Stack representation across models is one of the highest-divergence categories in engineering-hiring measurement. It is also one of the most closeable, once the source citation pattern is known.

## Post #20 — Friday, May 23

**Source:** Blog Post 15 (Staff Engineer journey, second pull)
**Template:** Question
**Hormozi optimization:** Hook = direct address to the named buyer; Retain = the specific candidate behavior that makes the question worth asking; Reward = a five-minute exercise the reader can complete today that produces signal.
**Compliance pass:** RULE-01 (positions around what the reader can observe, not against any vendor or audit), RULE-02, RULE-10 (engineering-specific exception — topic is genuinely tech), RULE-11, RULE-20, RULE-21, RULE-22

---

For engineering leaders and chief HR officers running engineering hiring:

When was the last time you opened ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity and asked each of them what they know about engineering at your company?

Most senior engineering candidates do exactly that within five minutes of receiving a recruiter outreach. The four answers will not match. One will describe the stack accurately and cite the engineering blog. One will return a generic "modern web technologies" answer. One will name a former senior engineer whose Substack post is currently the highest-weighted source on engineering culture at the company. One may not return a coherent answer at all.

Whichever answer the candidate happens to read first is the picture of the engineering organization they form before deciding whether to reply to the recruiter.

The exercise takes about ten minutes and costs nothing. It will not produce a structured measurement — the variance you observe is impressionistic, not scored, and the patterns that matter live below the surface of a single question. But it will produce a first read on whether the AI surface for your engineering organization is in the shape you assume it is.

For most companies, the answer is no.
