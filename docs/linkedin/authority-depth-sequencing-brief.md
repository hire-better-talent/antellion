## Per-slot brief

### Slot #22 — Wed May 27 — Authority depth as the next layer on top of the taxonomy

**Template:** Data Point
**Hook–Retain–Reward structure:**

- **Hook** (first line, attention test): name a specific surface category from yesterday's Post 16 and pair it with an observation about depth, not just presence. The line should make the reader pause on "wait, is presence not the right unit?"
- **Retain** (middle): introduce the depth vocabulary — Anchored / Present / Thin / Absent — and name what the three sub-components are at one line of resolution (density, voice diversity, recency). Plain language, no jargon.
- **Reward** (close): a single sentence that lands the implication for the reader — there are companies that look "present" on a surface but are actually thin in citable depth, and a depth read tells them apart from companies that are anchored. Punch-line close, no link.

**Angle direction (do not draft, this is a brief):** "Most employer brand audits ask whether a company is present on Glassdoor. The harder question — which actually predicts what AI cites when a candidate asks about that company — is how deep the authority on Glassdoor is. Density of distinct cited material, number of named voices, recency of what AI is pulling from. Same surface, four different depth tiers, four different conversations."

**Compliance pass:**

- RULE-01: framing is additive — "the harder question is X" not "audits miss X." Adjust on draft if drift creeps in.
- RULE-02: any explicit measurement claim uses the canonical phrase. If the post needs to say "we measure," it says "we measure authority depth across the AI-cited candidate-intent surfaces in the scored corpus."
- RULE-10: rotate sector — use a non-tech example for the worked illustration (regional bank, multi-state credit union, specialty retailer)
- RULE-20: no Antellion URL. Punch-line close, no CTA.
- RULE-22: no engagement gimmicks.
- No scan-runner framing: not "I ran a scan and noticed" — instead "Same surface, four different depth tiers" (structural framing of model behavior, not first-person operating).

### Slot #25 — Wed Jun 3 — A specific authority-depth observation from current scan work

**Template:** Data Point (real-time observation)
**Hook–Retain–Reward structure:**

- **Hook:** name a specific candidate persona and a specific surface, and report the depth tier observed in this week's scan work — without naming the company. The specificity of persona + surface + tier is what makes the reader read the second line.
- **Retain:** explain what produced the tier — number of distinct named voices observed, recency of the most-recent cited piece, density of cited material. Plain language. One paragraph maximum.
- **Reward:** name the structural implication — at this depth tier, AI's answers to candidate-intent queries on this persona and surface are predictable in shape, and changing the answer requires moving the depth, not the published material. Close on the observation, no CTA.

**Angle direction:** "A senior customer success persona at a late-stage SaaS company we scanned this week scored Thin on the Persona-Specific Community Platforms surface category — meaning AI was citing one Gainsight Community thread from 2024, no other named voices, and no fresh material in the last twelve months. The candidate-intent answer was about that one thread. Depth is the question, not presence."

**Compliance pass:**

- RULE-01: additive, no comparison.
- RULE-02: scoring claim scoped. The word "scored" + "in the scored corpus" or "in this week's scan corpus" anywhere it appears.
- RULE-10: sector-rotated (this slot lands customer success); follow-up depth posts rotate to revenue, operations, finance.
- No scan-runner framing: the observation is framed as the company's read, not the founder's experience. "Scored Thin" rather than "I scored this company as Thin."
- ST risk neutralization: the observation is methodologically specific (named voices, recency, density). Replicating it requires committing to hiring-specific scope and the twelve-category taxonomy.

### Slot #33 — Thu Jun 25 — Authority depth as a measurement category, framework form (capstone teaser by reference)

**Template:** Framework
**Hook–Retain–Reward structure:**

- **Hook:** open with the category claim — "Authority depth on the AI-cited candidate-intent surfaces is a measurable thing, not a vibe." Lands the noun in the first line.
- **Retain:** name the three sub-components, the four display tiers, and the structural reason depth matters more than presence (presence on a surface with one named voice and three-year-old material reads differently to AI than presence with eight named voices and material in the last six months). One paragraph each.
- **Reward:** the reward is the framework itself — the reader leaves with a measurement category they can ask their employer brand or comms team about. Bridge to next Tuesday's capstone in the punch line: "Pattern across companies is in the H1 2026 benchmark publishing Tuesday."

**Angle direction:** "Authority depth across the twelve citation-source categories AI reads for hiring queries is a category we measure. Density of cited material, voice diversity (how many distinct named humans authored what AI is pulling from), recency. Four tiers — Anchored, Present, Thin, Absent. Same company can be Anchored on Careers Site and Thin on every Specialist surface relevant to its highest-volume hiring persona. The asymmetry is the finding."

**Compliance pass:**

- RULE-01: additive throughout. The framework is what depth measurement *is*; nothing about what other frames *aren't*.
- RULE-02: canonical phrase used. "Across the AI-cited candidate-intent surfaces" appears at least once in the body, never "across the web."
- RULE-10: sector example is non-tech in the body (industrial distributor, regional health system, regional bank — rotate).
- RULE-20: no Antellion URL. Capstone reference is "publishing Tuesday" without a link.
- No scan-runner framing.
- ST risk neutralization: methodology specificity is the differentiator. The three sub-components and the four tiers are precise enough that comparable framework would need to commit to the same hiring-specific scope.
