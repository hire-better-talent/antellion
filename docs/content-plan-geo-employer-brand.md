# Content Plan: Owning the "GEO for Employer Brand" Narrative

**Date:** April 4, 2026
**Author:** Growth Operator
**Objective:** Establish Antellion as the definitive authority on how companies appear in AI when candidates decide where to work -- before SEO-adjacent competitors (Opinly.ai, etc.) claim this territory with blog content and no product.

---

## Strategic Thesis

There is a narrow window -- roughly 6-12 months -- where the "GEO for employer brand" concept is emerging but unowned. Several dynamics are converging:

1. **SEO tools are approaching from the search side.** Opinly.ai and others are publishing about Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) as a general concept. Their frame is search marketing. They have blog content but no employer-specific product.

2. **Employer branding agencies have not noticed yet.** Universum, Employer Brand Labs, and the Glassdoor/Comparably ecosystem still operate in the review-and-survey paradigm. AI visibility is not on their radar.

3. **Antellion has the only shipping product.** A structured seven-layer methodology, a candidate decision journey framework, confidence scoring, citation ecosystem mapping, and executive-grade reports. This is not a concept -- it is a delivered service.

The content strategy is not "publish a lot about GEO." It is: **define the vocabulary, publish the frameworks, produce the data, and become the source that AI models cite when anyone asks about this topic.** This is meta-GEO -- using the strategy we sell to market the strategy we sell.

## Part 1: The Antellion Terminology Framework

Before publishing anything, Antellion needs to own the vocabulary. Whoever names the concepts controls the category. Every content piece should use these terms consistently, repeatedly, and definitionally -- so that AI models associate the terms with Antellion.

### Terms Antellion Should Define and Own

| Term | Definition | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **AI Employer Visibility** | The degree to which a company appears, is described accurately, and is positioned favorably in AI-generated responses to candidate-intent queries. | This is the category name. Not "employer GEO." Not "talent SEO." AI Employer Visibility is what we measure. |
| **Candidate Decision Journey** | The four-stage process (Discovery, Consideration, Evaluation, Commitment) that models how candidates evaluate employers through AI. | This is Antellion's proprietary framework. It maps directly to the product's assessment methodology. |
| **Citation Ecosystem** | The set of platforms, publications, and data sources that AI models draw from when synthesizing employer-related answers. Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, Blind, Built In, LinkedIn, etc. | This reframes "backlinks" into something specific to employer perception in AI. |
| **Citation Gap** | A platform or source that AI cites when describing competitors but not the client. The highest-priority remediation target. | Specific, actionable, commercially useful. Every assessment finds these. |
| **Visibility Displacement** | When a competitor appears in an AI response where the client does not -- measured per decision stage and per query theme. | This is the competitive anxiety trigger. More precise than "outranked." |
| **AI Mention Rate** | The percentage of candidate-intent AI queries in which a company is named. The top-line metric of AI employer visibility. | Simple, quotable, comparable. "Your AI mention rate is 47%." |
| **Narrative Positioning Tier** | How AI frames a company at each decision stage: Champion, Contender, Peripheral, Cautionary, or Invisible. | This gives buyers a single word to describe their situation. "We're Peripheral at Discovery." |
| **Answer-Surface Intelligence** | The practice of analyzing and optimizing for AI answer surfaces (synthesized responses) rather than traditional search result pages. | This distinguishes the discipline from SEO. AI does not rank pages. It synthesizes answers. |
| **Pipeline Throughput Leakage** | The compounded loss of candidates across decision stages due to AI visibility gaps. A 50% drop at each stage means only 6% of AI-researching candidates reach the application stage. | This is the CFO/board-level framing. It connects visibility to pipeline math. |
| **Employer Signal Surface** | The aggregate of all digital signals (reviews, profiles, content, citations) that AI models synthesize when answering candidate queries about an employer. | This replaces "digital footprint" with something specific to AI synthesis. |

### How to Deploy the Framework

1. **Glossary page on the website.** A standalone, crawlable page that defines every term. This is the page AI models will cite when someone asks "what is AI employer visibility?"
2. **Consistent use in every content piece.** Never use a generic synonym when the Antellion term exists. Always "citation gap," never "missing source." Always "candidate decision journey," never "hiring funnel."
3. **First-use definitions in external publications.** When writing for ERE or LinkedIn, define the term on first use with a brief parenthetical. This teaches the reader and trains the model.
4. **Internal language discipline.** Every outreach email, every proposal, every slide deck uses these terms. Consistency across surfaces is how terminology becomes standard.

#### Piece 7: "GEO Is Not SEO: Why Generative Engine Optimization Requires a Different Playbook for Employer Brand"

**Format:** Blog post (1,500-2,000 words)
**Where to publish:** Company blog, LinkedIn article, pitch to TLNT or HR Technologist
**Target audience:** Employer Brand leaders, digital marketing leads, CMO
**Key message:** SEO optimizes for page rankings. GEO for employer brand optimizes for answer synthesis. The strategies, platforms, and metrics are fundamentally different. Companies that apply SEO thinking to AI visibility will waste their effort.
**AEO/GEO optimization angle:** When someone asks "what is the difference between GEO and SEO?" or "does SEO work for AI visibility?" this piece should be cited. The direct comparison format is highly citable. Use a structured comparison table.
**Content approach:** Three concrete differences, each with an example:
1. **Surface:** SEO targets page rankings. GEO targets synthesized answers. AI does not rank pages -- it reads hundreds of sources and generates one answer. There is no "page 1."
2. **Signals:** SEO signals are backlinks, keywords, page speed. GEO signals for employer brand are citation frequency, platform presence, narrative consistency, and sentiment patterns across the citation ecosystem.
3. **Measurement:** SEO measures click-through rate and ranking position. AI employer visibility measures mention rate, positioning tier, citation coverage, and competitive displacement per decision stage.
End with: "This is why we do not call what we do 'employer SEO.' It is a different surface with different rules."
**Priority:** Publish in week 4. This piece directly addresses the competitive narrative from SEO-adjacent tools.

- **Evaluation (Days 30-60):** Create content that addresses direct comparison queries. Ensure presence on platforms where competitors appear and you do not (citation gaps).
- **Commitment (Days 45-90):** Publish detailed interview process content, hiring timeline information, and role-specific details that AI can surface when candidates ask "what is the interview process at [Company]?"
Include measurement: "Re-scan your AI visibility at Day 90 to measure what moved."
**Constraint:** Only recommend actions that are consistent with what Antellion actually recommends in its assessment reports. Do not invent new remediation categories.

### LinkedIn Cadence (Founder's Personal Account)

LinkedIn is the primary organic distribution channel. The founder should post 3-4 times per week using this cadence:

| Day | Content type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | **Data point** from a real scan (anonymized) | "We scanned how AI describes employers in fintech to candidates. Company A: mentioned in 72% of Discovery queries. Company B (same size, same city): 18%. The gap traced to 3 platforms. Company B had no presence on any of them." |

**LinkedIn post rules:**
- Lead with a specific data point or observation, never with a generic claim about AI.
- Never say "AI is disrupting..." or "the future of..." -- these are commodity phrases that enterprise buyers skip.
- Every post should teach something the reader did not know. If the post does not pass this test, do not publish it.
- Include the Antellion framework terms naturally. Do not force them.
- Do not pitch the product in posts. The product sells itself when the content establishes the problem.

## Part 4: AEO/GEO Meta-Strategy

This section addresses the meta-goal: making Antellion's content the content that AI models cite when anyone asks about this topic.

### What makes AI models cite a source

Based on observable patterns in how current AI models (GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini) select and cite sources:

1. **Definitional authority.** Content that defines terms clearly and concisely gets cited when those terms are queried. This is why the glossary (Piece 3) and the cornerstone article (Piece 1) are the highest-priority AEO assets.

2. **Original data.** Content with specific numbers, methodology, and findings that do not exist elsewhere gets cited because AI has no alternative source. This is why the research report (Piece 6/13) is critical.

3. **Structured format.** Content with clear H2/H3 headers, tables, bullet points, and direct question-answer patterns is easier for AI models to extract and cite. Every piece should use these formatting patterns.

4. **Publication context.** Content published on established editorial platforms (ERE, SHRM, Medium) carries more weight than content on a new company blog. This is why the external placement strategy (Piece 15) matters disproportionately for AEO.

5. **Consistency across surfaces.** When AI encounters the same terms and frameworks across multiple sources (company blog, LinkedIn, ERE, Medium), it treats those terms as established vocabulary. This is why terminology consistency matters so much.

### The self-reinforcing loop

The content plan creates a self-reinforcing dynamic:

1. Antellion publishes content that defines AI employer visibility.
2. AI models index and cite that content.
3. When prospects ask AI about employer visibility in AI, Antellion's content (and terminology) appears in the answer.
4. Prospects encounter Antellion's framework before they encounter any competitor.
5. Antellion's scans reveal whether its own content is being cited -- and if not, the remediation playbook applies to Antellion's own content strategy.

This is meta-GEO. Antellion is its own first case study.

## Part 6: Content Production Constraints and Guardrails

### What the founder can realistically produce

Antellion is pre-revenue and founder-led. There is no content team. This plan assumes:

- **Founder writes 1 blog post per week** (1,500-2,500 words). This is aggressive but feasible if the posts are data-driven (the data comes from actual scans, which the founder is already running).
- **Founder writes 3-4 LinkedIn posts per week** (150-300 words each). These should take 15-20 minutes each if they are drawn from real scan observations.
- **Guest article pitches happen in batches** (pitch 4 publications in one sitting, then wait for responses).
- **The research report is accumulated, not written in one sprint.** Data is gathered over weeks 1-8, then organized into a report in weeks 9-10.

### Quality threshold

Every piece must pass this test before publishing: "Would a VP of Talent Acquisition at a 2,000-person company read this and think 'this person understands my problem'?"

If the answer is no -- if the piece reads like generic AI commentary, SaaS marketing filler, or a blog post written to fill a content calendar -- do not publish it. One sharp, specific, data-driven article per week is worth more than three fluffy posts.

### What not to publish

- **Product announcement posts** ("Introducing Antellion! We do X!"). No one cares about a new product. They care about the problem it solves.
- **Generic AI trend commentary** ("AI is changing how candidates research employers"). This is obvious. Add specific data or do not publish.
- **Capability claims that exceed the product.** If multi-model scanning is not shipped, do not write about "scanning across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude." Write about what the data shows from the scans you have actually run.
- **Content that uses "transform," "revolutionize," "game-changing," or "disruptive."** Enterprise buyers tune this out. Use specific, concrete language.

### Measuring what works

| Metric | What it tells you | How to track |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn post impressions and engagement | Whether the ICP is paying attention | LinkedIn analytics |
| Blog traffic to cornerstone + glossary pages | Whether AI is driving referral traffic | Google Analytics / Vercel Analytics |
| Inbound "how did you find us?" mentions of AI or content | Whether content is driving discovery | Track in sales conversations |
| AI citation of Antellion content | Whether the meta-GEO strategy is working | Run periodic scans of "what is AI employer visibility?" queries across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity |
| Guest article acceptance rate | Whether external publications find the content credible | Track pitch outcomes |
| Snapshot requests attributed to content | Whether content drives commercial interest | Track inbound requests |

## Part 7: Competitive Response Playbook

### If Opinly.ai or another SEO tool publishes "GEO for Employer Brand" content

**Do not engage directly.** Do not name them. Do not write "why we're different from Opinly." Instead:

1. **Publish a response piece** that demonstrates deeper expertise. If they write about GEO for employer brand at a surface level, publish a piece with real data, specific platform analysis, and the candidate decision journey framework. Let the depth difference speak for itself.

2. **Emphasize what they cannot show.** SEO tools do not have candidate-intent query taxonomies, decision journey frameworks, citation ecosystem maps, or competitive displacement analysis for employer brand. They have blog posts. Antellion has a methodology and a product.

3. **Use their content as signal.** If SEO tools are blogging about GEO for employer brand, it validates the category. Reference the trend ("the concept of Generative Engine Optimization is gaining attention") and then narrow it to Antellion's specific expertise ("for employer brand specifically, the methodology requires understanding candidate decision journeys, not just search intent").

### If an employer branding agency starts offering AI visibility services

**This is validation.** Respond by publishing data that demonstrates the difference between an agency's subjective assessment and a structured, scored, confidence-rated methodology. The key differentiator: agencies will offer AI visibility as one slide in a broader engagement. Antellion offers it as a complete, auditable diagnostic with a seven-layer methodology. The comparison favors specificity.
