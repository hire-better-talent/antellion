# Sales Walkthrough — Authority Surface Map

**Use case:** founder discovery call with a CHRO, CPO, or CMO who has seen the Diagnostic Report and is asking, in some form, "what does the Authority Surface Map section actually tell me?"
**Length:** five sentences. Read aloud time: ~45 seconds.
**Language register:** plain. No HR acronyms without spell-out. No "AI authority" without scoping qualifier.

---

## The five-sentence walkthrough

> The Authority Surface Map looks at the twelve citation-source categories AI draws from when it answers a candidate's question about working at your company, and for each one it records how dense your authority is, how many distinct named voices are speaking for you on it, and how recently the cited material was written. We score depth across the AI-cited candidate-intent surfaces in your scored corpus — not authority across the web — and the output is a four-tier read on each of the twelve categories: Anchored, Present, Thin, or Absent. The point of the section is not the score; it's the asymmetry — almost every company we measure is Anchored on two or three surfaces, Thin or Absent on the rest, and the Thin-or-Absent surfaces are usually the ones the most relevant candidate persona is reading first. The finding type that comes out of this section is called an Authority Deficit, and each one names a specific surface, quantifies the depth gap, and selects a remediation category, so the section is something an employer brand team can act on rather than just review. What the Diagnostic gives you is the photograph of where your authority sits today; what changes month-to-month after that — and whether your surface investments are moving the score — is the question the Continuous engagement is built to answer.

---

## Why each sentence does what it does

| Sentence | Purpose | Compliance note |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Defines the unit of analysis (the twelve categories) and the three sub-components (density, voice diversity, recency) in one breath | Lands "twelve categories" early so the buyer has the structural anchor before any score language |
| 2 | Establishes scope (the scored corpus, not the web) and the display vocabulary (Anchored / Present / Thin / Absent) | Canonical phrase used verbatim — "across the AI-cited candidate-intent surfaces in your scored corpus." RULE-02 lock |
| 3 | Names the structural pattern (asymmetry) — gives the buyer the headline observation without naming any company | No comparative-without-replacement. "Most companies we measure" is corpus-grounded, not a generic claim |
| 4 | Names the finding type (Authority Deficit) and the three-criteria test (specific surface, quantified gap, remediation category) | Mirrors the material-finding bar from Post 18. Reinforces actionability |
| 5 | Bridges to Continuous via the "photograph vs. tracked curve" frame — without selling Continuous in the discovery call | "Built to answer" not "monitors" or "tracks" or "watches AI for you" — keeps Continuous in the intelligence-service register |

---

## Things the walkthrough deliberately does NOT say

- "We measure your AI authority." — too web-wide, triggers RULE-02. The canonical phrase scopes the claim.
- "Other audits miss this." — direct comparative-without-replacement, RULE-01 violation. The walkthrough lands on what the section *is*, not on what other audits *aren't*.
- "We change what AI says about you." — never. Antellion measures and surfaces deficits; remediation work changes what AI synthesizes, and that work is the buyer's (or a Phase 2 engagement's).
- "Our AI agent monitors your AI visibility." — collapses the category. The walkthrough closes on "the Continuous engagement" — analyst-led, founder-signed, agent-assisted production.
- "I ran a scan and noticed…" — no scan-runner framing. The walkthrough is in the Diagnostic Report's voice, not the founder's first-person operating voice.
