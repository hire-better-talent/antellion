# Baseline + Action Brief

**Tier:** Feature Downsell — between Phase 1 Baseline and Phase 2 Focused
**Fee:** $24,000 total
**Delivery:** 3 weeks from deposit (2 weeks Baseline + 1 week Action Brief)
**Delivered by:** Hire Better Talent LLC, d/b/a Antellion
**Status:** Active — available as a scoped response when a Phase 2 pitch encounters budget resistance
**Last updated:** 2026-04-23

---

## Purpose

A single-SOW variant of Phase 1 Baseline that adds one additional deliverable — a 15-page prioritized remediation brief for one candidate journey stage of the customer's choosing — and catches Phase 2 deals that stall between Baseline ($14K) and Focused ($89K) because the customer wants more than Baseline but cannot approve a $75K+ Phase 2 budget this quarter.

It preserves the rollover path: the $24K credits 100% toward Focused, Strategic, or Enterprise within 60 days, so adding the Action Brief does not cost the customer future Phase 2 budget.

---

## What's the same as Baseline ($14,000)

All Phase 1 Baseline deliverables are included without modification:

1. Full-journey AI analysis — 300-400 queries across Discovery, Consideration, Evaluation, and Commitment stages, tested across ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini
2. Competitive citation map against 3 named competitors
3. Source attribution analysis
4. **Journey Action Map** — 1-2 page strategic scorecard covering all four candidate journey stages
5. Prioritized remediation roadmap — at least 5 platform-level actions
6. Executive-ready written report
7. 60-minute executive readout call
8. One 30-minute follow-up Q&A within 30 days
9. All Baseline completeness criteria and guarantees

---

## What's added — the Action Brief

A **15-page prioritized remediation brief** for **one candidate journey stage** (customer selects Discovery, Consideration, Evaluation, or Commitment after reviewing the Baseline findings).

### Brief structure

| Section | Pages |
|---|---|
| 1. Stage context and findings synthesis from Baseline | 2 |
| 2. Named platforms ranked by remediation priority (5-8 platforms) | 3 |
| 3. Content gap inventory — what AI is not citing that it should be | 3 |
| 4. Per-platform remediation recommendation (specific content type, frequency, owner role) | 4 |
| 5. 90-day execution sequence — week-by-week prioritization | 2 |
| 6. Measurement plan — what to re-scan in 90 days and what success looks like | 1 |

### Methodology depth for the Action Brief

- Deep dive into one stage's citation ecosystem (vs Baseline's full-journey overview)
- Per-platform remediation direction with named platform owners (e.g., "Glassdoor Employer Brand, owner typically TA Ops Director")
- Content gap inventory cross-referenced against AI-cited sources for the three named competitors
- Integration plan designed to complement existing careers site, employer brand, and recruitment marketing programs — not replace them

### When the customer picks the stage

After the Baseline executive readout, the customer has 5 business days to select which journey stage the Action Brief will cover. Antellion delivers the Action Brief within 1 week of the stage selection.

## What's added — the Action Brief readout

- **Additional 45-minute Action Brief readout call** with the analyst (separate from the 60-minute Baseline readout)
- Attended by up to 5 customer team members (typically includes TA Ops, employer brand, and recruitment marketing leads alongside the CHRO or VP TA sponsor)
- Walkthrough of the 90-day execution sequence with Q&A

## Rollover

**100% of the $24,000 fee credits toward Focused ($75,000 Phase 2), Strategic ($90,000 Phase 2), or Enterprise ($110,000+ Phase 2) engagements signed within 60 days of Action Brief delivery.**

Example: A customer who completes Baseline + Action Brief at $24K and then commits to Focused within 60 days pays **$75,000 - $24,000 = $51,000 for the Phase 2 upgrade** ($89,000 - $14,000 Baseline - $24,000 Action Brief credit = $51K incremental).

Rollover is executed as a credit memo in the Phase 2 SOW. If the customer does not proceed within 60 days, the credit expires.

## When to offer Baseline + Action Brief

### Offer it when

- The customer pitches toward Focused, Strategic, or Enterprise and signals price resistance specifically tied to the current budget cycle ("we could do $30K this quarter but not $90K")
- The customer has read the Baseline spec, understands the methodology, and asks "what do I do with this once I have it?" — signaling they want one concrete execution path, not a full Phase 2
- The customer has a specific journey stage they are most anxious about (usually Consideration or Evaluation) and they want a plan for that stage specifically
- The customer's procurement cycle requires a sub-$30K engagement ceiling this quarter but they have authority to commit to a Phase 2 rollover in Q+1

### Do NOT lead with it

- **Never present this before Focused, Strategic, or Enterprise.** Leading with Baseline + Action Brief trains the buyer to anchor at $24K, which collapses the deal into a discretionary-spend zone and damages Phase 2 conversion.
- Not appropriate as a Phase 1 default. Phase 1 Baseline alone is the standard Phase 1 offer.
- Not appropriate for customers with the budget and trigger events to justify Phase 2. Those customers should be pushed toward Focused minimum — they will not get the full value of their investment in a single-stage brief.
- Not appropriate when the customer has not yet seen Baseline findings. The Action Brief's stage selection requires Baseline output to be grounded.

### When a sales rep should escalate before offering

- Customer is F500 / $1B+ revenue. They have the budget for Focused; the procurement problem is usually timing, not amount. Jordan should confirm before offering the downsell that procurement-cycle timing is the real blocker.
- Customer has named a board-level or activist-investor trigger event. These are Board-Level or Enterprise customers; do not downsell them.

## SOW structure

Single SOW, executed under the Antellion MSA. Uses a modified Scope Block A (Baseline) plus an appended Action Brief section.

### Fee and schedule

- **Total Fee:** $24,000
- **Deposit:** $12,000 (Net 15 from SOW execution)
- **Balance:** $12,000 (Net 30 from Action Brief acceptance)
- **Delivery timeline:** 3 weeks total (2 weeks Baseline + 1 week Action Brief, beginning after customer stage selection)

### Guarantee

Inherits all Baseline completeness criteria, plus:

- Action Brief must include at least **8 platform-specific recommendations** covering the selected journey stage, each with named platform, content type, and owner role
- Action Brief must include a 90-day execution sequence with at least 6 scheduled milestones

If the Action Brief fails either criterion, customer receives a **full refund of the $10,000 incremental over Baseline** (not the entire $24K) within 10 business days.

### Factual accuracy

Same as all tiers: any factual error corrected within 48 hours or refunded.

## Symphony Talent / employer brand additive framing

In any conversation or SOW language describing this tier, reinforce that the Action Brief is designed to **direct and sharpen the customer's existing employer brand, careers site, and recruitment marketing work** — not replace it. Common positioning language:

- "The Action Brief tells your employer brand agency exactly which platforms AI is citing for your competitors and not for you, so their existing content calendar can be re-sequenced around the AI citation gaps."
- "The 90-day execution sequence is designed for your in-house recruitment marketing team to run alongside their current campaigns, not instead of them."
- "Think of the Action Brief as a strategic measurement layer above your brand program. It doesn't change who produces content — it changes which content gets prioritized this quarter."
