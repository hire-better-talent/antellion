# Fixtures — RULE-01: Symphony Talent additive

Antellion never anti-sells employer brand. Founder's day job is at Symphony Talent. Anti-employer-brand framing creates a personal conflict of interest and contradicts Antellion's structural positioning as a measurement layer above existing employer brand programs.

## Known-bad (must FAIL)

> Stop investing in employer brand and start investing in AI visibility. The old playbook is dead.

> Your employer brand program is yesterday's solution. AI is what shapes candidate decisions now.

> Recruitment marketing is dying. The companies winning are skipping it entirely and going direct to AI.

> Why are you still paying an employer brand agency when AI will tell candidates about you whether you do or not?

> The Glassdoor era is over. Time to retire your employer brand budget.

## Known-good (must PASS)

> AI visibility is a measurement layer above your employer brand program — not a replacement.

> The employer brand work most companies are doing is real. The new layer is that AI synthesizes from inputs nobody's team has been formally measured on for the last decade.

> Your employer brand investment is real and serious. Antellion measures what AI is currently citing, so the work your brand teams already do gets directed with more precision.

> A strong employer brand and strong AI employer visibility are two different measurements. Most companies have the first. Almost none are measuring the second.

## Edge cases (judgment)

> Candidate perception is changing — what worked in employer brand five years ago doesn't always show up in AI today.

*Read:* should pass. Frames change as evolution, not replacement. The phrase "what worked five years ago doesn't always show up" is an observation about coverage, not a deprecation of employer brand.

> Stop measuring employer brand the way you measured it in 2020.

*Read:* ambiguous. "Stop measuring" can read as anti-employer-brand if the reader doesn't catch the "the way you measured it" qualifier. Recommend tightening to "Add a new measurement layer" or "Update how you measure employer brand to include AI surfaces." Mark as AMBIGUOUS or WARNING; flag for founder review.

> Your employer brand agency can't do this work — they're not built for it.

*Read:* should fail. "Can't do this work" reads as a deprecation even if the literal claim is about scope (AI visibility ≠ employer brand). The way to express the same point: "Your employer brand agency is the right partner for the work they do; AI visibility is a different measurement surface." See `docs/blog/geo-vs-seo-employer-brand.md` for the canonical phrasing of this distinction.
