# Calendar Blocks — Launch Week and Beyond

Non-negotiable calendar discipline for the first two weeks after the Apr 29 launch. Instantly sends automatically — these blocks exist so YOU are available to respond fast when replies land. A 2-hour response time on a real CTO reply is the difference between a Snapshot request and a dead thread.

All times Eastern. Adjust if most of your prospects are in Pacific/Central.

---

## Recurring events to create in Google Calendar

### 1. Send Window — "Antellion: Instantly auto-send"

**Recurrence:** Tue, Wed, Thu — weekly
**Time:** 7:30 AM – 8:30 AM ET
**Color:** Orange (high priority)
**Notification:** 10 min before

**Description to paste into the event:**
> Instantly sends the next batch at 7:30 AM ET per prospect local time. Do not schedule meetings or deep work during this window. Be at a device with Instantly open.
>
> Tasks:
> - Open Instantly dashboard
> - Confirm today's batch actually sent (check for any bounces or errors)
> - Note the total sends for the daily tracker entry

### 2. Reply Monitoring — "Antellion: reply window"

**Recurrence:** Tue, Wed, Thu — weekly
**Time:** 8:30 AM – 10:00 AM ET
**Color:** Red (critical)
**Notification:** 5 min before

**Description:**
> First replies to morning sends usually arrive 30-90 min after delivery. Keep Instantly + email open. Respond to any reply within 2 hours per the response-templates.md playbook.
>
> Rule: every reply needs a human response within 2 hours. Template scaffolding is in docs/response-templates.md. Read the reply, choose the template, adjust one line, send.
>
> Record every reply in docs/outreach-tracker.csv immediately after responding.

### 3. Weekly Metrics Review — "Antellion: weekly review"

**Recurrence:** Friday — weekly
**Time:** 9:00 AM – 10:00 AM ET
**Color:** Blue
**Notification:** 15 min before

**Description:**
> Weekly metrics checkpoint. Use this hour to:
> - Pull Instantly metrics: sends, open rate, reply rate, positive reply rate, unsubscribes, bounces
> - Cross-reference with the tracker CSV
> - Check Warmbox deliverability dashboard — inbox placement rate, warmup score trend
> - Note any concerning signals (bounces > 2%, open rate < 30%, spam placement)
> - Plan next week's sends: batch size, industry focus, any A/B subject line winner to lock in
> - Update the session handoff memory with current state

### 4. Monday Prep — "Antellion: Monday prep"

**Recurrence:** Monday — weekly
**Time:** 7:30 AM – 8:30 AM ET
**Color:** Green
**Notification:** 10 min before

**Description:**
> Monday is a no-send day (Mondays perform poorly for enterprise inbox). Use this hour to:
> - Catch up on any weekend replies that came in
> - Review the week's upcoming batch in Instantly
> - Check the tracker CSV for any follow-ups due this week
> - Check Warmbox overnight activity — any spam moves or warmup score changes
> - Review blog publishing schedule (Blog #5 Apr 17 Fri, #6 Apr 22 Wed, #7 Apr 24 Fri)

### 5. Snapshot Fulfillment — "Antellion: Snapshot fulfillment"

**Recurrence:** Tue + Thu — weekly (add as needed when Snapshot requests come in)
**Time:** 2:00 PM – 4:00 PM ET
**Color:** Purple
**Notification:** 30 min before

**Description:**
> Block time to actually run Snapshots for prospects who requested them. Target: deliver within 48 hours of request.
>
> Workflow:
> 1. Create new Snapshot in operator dashboard with prospect info + competitors
> 2. Wait ~1 minute for scan execution
> 3. Review the findings card — spot-check excerpts for any red flags
> 4. Click "Copy prospect link"
> 5. Open the /s/{token} URL yourself to verify it renders cleanly
> 6. Reply to the prospect's email thread: "{{firstName}} — your Snapshot is ready: [link]. Happy to walk through it if useful."
> 7. Update tracker CSV with share link URL + delivery date

Remove this block from the calendar for any week with zero pending Snapshot requests.

## Rules for the first two weeks

1. **No external meetings Tue/Wed/Thu 7:30 AM – 10:00 AM.** Zero exceptions. This is the highest-leverage 7.5 hours per week until the Snapshot pipeline has proven itself.
2. **Check email before coffee on send days.** A 2-hour response window starts at 7:30 AM. You cannot afford to discover a reply at 10:30.
3. **Every reply gets logged in the tracker the same day.** If you wait, you forget, and the data you need in Week 3 evaporates.
4. **No new projects until Day 10.** The first 2 weeks are pure execution. Resist building anything, polishing anything, or optimizing anything. Just respond to replies, fulfill Snapshots, log data.
