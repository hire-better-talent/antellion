# Antellion Growth Playbook
## Conversion-Optimized Strategy Applied from Hormozi Frameworks

**Date:** March 2026
**Status:** Executable draft -- not a strategy deck. Every section contains specific actions, dollar amounts, and copy you can use today.

**Product Reality Check:** This playbook is written against Antellion's actual current capabilities: client/competitor management, query generation and clustering, semi-manual visibility scans with heuristic scoring, scan comparison and citation analysis, executive audit reports with prioritized recommendations, report export, and content asset tracking. Where the playbook references future capabilities (automated scans, self-serve dashboards), it is clearly marked. Nothing here fakes functionality.

## 1. POSITIONING & CATEGORY CREATION

### 1.1 The "Category of One"

Antellion is not an ATS. Not an employer branding agency. Not an SEO tool. Not Glassdoor monitoring. It is the first platform that shows companies exactly how AI describes them when candidates ask where to work.

**Category name:** AI Employer Visibility

This is the category. Not "employer brand intelligence" (too close to Glassdoor/Comparably). Not "talent SEO" (sounds like keyword stuffing for job boards). Not "AI talent analytics" (sounds like resume screening).

**The category-defining statement:**

> "Antellion is an AI employer visibility platform. We show you how ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity describe your company when candidates ask where to work -- and what to do when the answer isn't good enough."

This is the sentence that should survive every edit of every piece of copy. It does three things:
1. Names the specific AI models (concrete, not abstract)
2. Names the specific moment (candidate asking where to work)
3. Names the specific action (what to do about it)

### 1.2 Escaping Commodity Comparison

The danger zones:

| They will try to compare us to... | Our escape route |
|---|---|
| Employer branding agencies (Universum, Employer Brand Labs) | "Agencies help you tell your story. We show you what AI is already telling candidates about you -- whether you wrote it or not." |
| Glassdoor / Comparably / Blind | "Review sites show you what employees say. We show you what AI says -- and AI synthesizes hundreds of sources into the single answer candidates actually read." |
| SEO tools (Semrush, Ahrefs) | "SEO tools optimize for Google search rankings. AI models don't rank pages -- they synthesize answers. Different surface, different strategy." |
| HR tech platforms (Phenom, Beamery) | "HR tech manages your hiring workflow. We manage how you show up before the candidate ever reaches your workflow." |

**The framing that makes comparison collapse:**

"You spend $X on employer brand. You have no idea if AI is using any of it."

This is the sentence that kills comparison. It reframes the conversation from "which tool should we buy" to "there is a thing happening that none of your tools see."

### 1.3 Competitive Moat Narrative

The moat is not technology (anyone can query an LLM). The moat is:

1. **The query corpus.** Antellion generates candidate-intent query clusters mapped to real hiring scenarios -- role-specific, geography-specific, intent-classified. This is the proprietary lens. The value is not "we can ask ChatGPT a question" -- the value is "we know which 47 questions a senior backend engineer in Austin asks before deciding where to apply."

2. **The comparison methodology.** Visibility scores, sentiment scores, citation gap analysis, competitor mention rates -- these are structured, repeatable, and auditable. The report output is not a vibe check; it is a competitive intelligence document.

3. **The recommendation layer.** Citation gap domains, competitive positioning priorities, content gaps, sentiment remediation -- these are specific, actionable, and tied to scan data. They are not generic advice.

**Moat narrative for sales conversations:**

"Any consultant can Google your company. Antellion runs structured visibility scans across the exact queries your target candidates use, scores the results, compares you against named competitors, identifies which citation sources are helping them and not you, and produces an executive-ready audit with prioritized remediation. Try doing that in a spreadsheet every quarter."

## 2. GRAND SLAM OFFER DESIGN

### 2.1 Value Equation Applied

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)**

| Lever | What it means for Antellion | How to maximize it |
|---|---|---|
| **Dream Outcome** | "When candidates ask AI where to work, your company is the answer." | Frame the outcome as competitive advantage in talent acquisition, not just visibility. Visibility is the mechanism. Winning hires is the outcome. |
| **Perceived Likelihood** | The buyer needs to believe this will actually work for their company. | Lead with the audit. Show them their current score. Show them the competitor gap. Show them the citation sources they are missing. Data creates belief. |
| **Time Delay** | How fast do they see results? | First audit delivered in days, not months. Remediation roadmap is immediate. First visibility improvement can be measured in the next scan cycle. |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** | What does the buyer have to do? | Almost nothing. Antellion runs the scans, generates the queries, produces the report. The buyer's team reviews recommendations and decides what to act on. This is not a tool they have to learn -- it is intelligence they receive. |

### 2.2 The Grand Slam Offer Structure

**Core Offer: "AI Employer Visibility Audit"**

This is the wedge. Not a subscription. Not a platform login. An audit.

What the audit includes (these are all things the product actually does today):

1. **Company profile setup** -- client configuration with domain, industry, and competitor mapping
2. **Query cluster generation** -- 30-50 candidate-intent queries across 6 themes (reputation, compensation, hiring process, role expectations, culture, competitor comparison) tailored to the client's target roles and geography
3. **Visibility scans** -- each query run against major AI models, with response capture, mention detection, and citation extraction
4. **Competitive comparison** -- mention rates, visibility scores, and sentiment scores for the client vs. 3-5 named competitors
5. **Citation gap analysis** -- which domains and sources surface competitors but not the client
6. **Executive audit report** -- structured report with executive summary, visibility findings, competitor analysis, citation patterns, and prioritized recommendations
7. **Export-ready document** -- printable/PDF report formatted for board-level presentation

**Bonuses (things that add value without requiring new product work):**

- **Bonus 1: "Competitor Visibility Scorecards"** -- the same scan comparison data, but formatted as individual competitor breakdown cards. This is a reformatting of data the product already produces.
- **Bonus 2: "Citation Source Playbook"** -- the gap domains from the citation analysis, organized into an actionable list with source type (review site, blog, social profile, press) and recommended action. This is a repackaging of the citation analysis.
- **Bonus 3: "Query Intent Map"** -- the generated query clusters visualized by theme and intent, showing the client which candidate questions they are winning vs. losing. This is a reformatting of the query cluster data.

**Guarantee:**

"If the audit does not surface at least 3 competitive visibility gaps you were not previously aware of, the audit is free."

This is a safe guarantee because the product literally compares the client against competitors across dozens of queries. It will always surface gaps. But it sounds bold and it eliminates risk for the buyer.

**Scarcity:**

"We take 5 new audit clients per month." (Legitimate -- the scan process has a semi-manual component. This is capacity-based scarcity, not manufactured scarcity.)

**Urgency:**

"Every quarter you wait, AI models train on more data that does not include you. Your competitors' visibility compounds while yours stays flat." (True -- AI model training data has recency effects.)

### 2.3 Naming

**Do not call the audit an "audit."** Call it:

**"AI Employer Visibility Assessment"**

The word "assessment" is better than "audit" for enterprise buyers because:
- "Audit" implies something is wrong (defensive posture)
- "Assessment" implies strategic evaluation (proactive posture)
- Assessment is what consultants sell for $50K+

The full offer name:

**"Antellion AI Employer Visibility Assessment"**

The premium version:

**"Antellion Strategic Visibility Assessment"** -- includes the audit plus a 90-day remediation plan with quarterly re-scan.

### 2.4 Commodity vs. Grand Slam Contrast

**Commodity approach (what agencies sell today):**
- "We will improve your employer brand" -- vague outcome
- $15K-$40K for an EVP project that takes 3-6 months
- No measurement of AI specifically
- Deliverable is a slide deck with messaging pillars
- Buyer cannot prove ROI

**Antellion Grand Slam:**
- "We will show you exactly how AI describes you to candidates, where you trail competitors, which citation sources you are missing, and what to fix first" -- specific, measurable
- $5K-$15K for the assessment, delivered in 2-3 weeks
- Measured against named competitors on specific queries
- Deliverable is a data-driven executive report with prioritized recommendations
- Buyer can show the gap, show the plan, and re-measure next quarter

The Antellion offer is cheaper, faster, and more specific than the agency alternative. It also does something the agency literally cannot do.

## 3. MESSAGING & COPY PRINCIPLES

### 3.1 Core Pain Narrative

The pain is not "our employer brand is weak." The pain is more specific than that:

**"Candidates are asking AI where to work. AI is answering. You have no idea what it's saying about you."**

This is the pain that matters because:
1. It is new (did not exist 2 years ago)
2. It is invisible (no one is monitoring it)
3. It is competitive (AI is recommending someone -- and it might not be you)
4. It is compounding (every quarter you ignore it, AI gets more confident in its current answer)

**Pain hierarchy for different buyers:**

| Buyer | Primary pain | Secondary pain |
|---|---|---|
| VP Talent Acquisition | "We are losing candidates we never even see because AI does not recommend us." | "My cost-per-hire is rising and I cannot explain why." |
| Chief People Officer | "We spend millions on employer brand with no visibility into the AI channel." | "The board is asking about our AI strategy and I have nothing for the talent side." |
| Head of Employer Brand | "I built our EVP and I have no idea if AI is using any of it." | "Our competitors might be doing this and I would not know." |
| CMO | "We control our search presence, our social presence, our media presence. We do not control our AI presence." | "This is a brand risk we are not measuring." |

### 3.2 Language That Creates Urgency Without Being Salesy

Enterprise buyers recoil from fake urgency. These work because they are true:

- "AI models are forming opinions about your company right now. You just cannot see them."
- "Your competitors' visibility compounds. Yours does not -- unless you are actively managing it."
- "The candidate who asked ChatGPT about your company yesterday got an answer. You do not know what it was."
- "By the time you notice the pipeline problem, the AI perception gap has been compounding for quarters."

**Do NOT say:**
- "Act now before it is too late" (generic)
- "Limited time offer" (enterprise buyers will walk away)
- "AI is disrupting everything" (commodity AI hype)
- "Transform your talent strategy" (means nothing)

### 3.3 Dream Outcome in Enterprise Language

The dream outcome is not "better employer brand." The dream outcome, in language a CPO would use with their board:

> "We have visibility into how AI-driven candidate discovery surfaces our company relative to competitors, with a structured remediation plan that improves our positioning each quarter."

Shorter version for outreach:

> "Know how AI describes you to candidates. Fix what it gets wrong."

Even shorter for ads/social:

> "Control your AI employer story."

### 3.4 Proof Elements That Increase Perceived Likelihood

Since Antellion is early-stage, proof comes from specificity, not from logos:

1. **The live audit.** The single most powerful proof element. Run a partial visibility scan for the prospect's company during the sales conversation. Show them their mention rate. Show them a competitor beating them. This is not a mock -- it is real data from the product.

2. **The methodology.** Walk the buyer through how query clusters are generated (6 intent themes, role-specific, geography-specific). Walk them through the scoring (visibility, sentiment, citation gaps). The more specific the methodology, the more credible the product.

3. **The report.** Show a real (or realistic demo) executive audit report. The report format itself -- executive summary, visibility findings, competitor analysis, citation patterns, prioritized recommendations -- signals enterprise seriousness.

4. **Competitor mention rates.** "Deloitte has a 73% AI mention rate for consulting roles. You have 31%." A single data point like this is worth more than ten slides.

5. **Citation gap specificity.** "Glassdoor, Built In, and Blind all surface your competitor but not you." Naming the actual sources makes the gap tangible.

## 4. LEAD GENERATION & CONVERSION ENGINE

### 4.1 Core Four Prioritization

**Priority order for Antellion's current stage:**

**1st: Cold Outreach (free, 1:1)** -- START HERE.

Why: Enterprise deals close through relationships, not inbound. The product's audit-led sales motion is perfectly suited to cold outreach because you can lead with a real data point about the prospect's company.

The play:
- Run a quick visibility scan for the prospect's company on 5-10 queries
- Lead the outreach with a specific finding ("ChatGPT mentions Datadog 3x more than [Prospect Company] in DevOps hiring queries")
- Offer the full assessment as the CTA

Volume target: 50 personalized outreach sequences per week. Each one takes 15-20 minutes because you are running a real scan snippet.

**2nd: Post Free Content (free, 1:many)** -- LAYER IN MONTH 2.

Why: The category does not exist yet. You have to create demand by creating awareness. Content is how you establish "AI employer visibility" as a concept buyers recognize.

Content themes (detailed in 4.5 below).

**3rd: Warm Outreach (free, 1:1)** -- ACTIVATE AS CLIENTS CLOSE.

Why: Every completed assessment generates 3-5 warm referral opportunities because the buyer shows the report to peers at other companies. But you need clients first.

**4th: Run Paid Ads (paid, 1:many)** -- NOT YET.

Why: The category is too new. Paid ads work for known-category demand capture. There is no "AI employer visibility" search volume yet. Paid comes after content creates the category awareness.

### 4.2 The Lead Magnet

**"AI Employer Visibility Snapshot"**

This is the narrow, specific problem solved completely:

> "We ran your company through 10 AI candidate queries and scored the results. Here is your AI Employer Visibility Snapshot -- your mention rate, your sentiment, and the 3 biggest gaps vs. competitors. Free. No call required."

What it includes:
- Client mention rate across 10 queries (real data from a quick scan)
- Top competitor mention rate (real data)
- Sentiment summary (positive/negative/neutral)
- 3 specific citation gaps
- 1-page PDF, branded

What it does NOT include (creating the upsell):
- Full query cluster analysis
- All competitor comparisons
- Prioritized recommendations
- Remediation plan
- Quarterly tracking

**This lead magnet works because:**
1. It solves a narrow problem completely (what does AI say about me?)
2. It uses real data, not generic advice
3. It takes 30-60 minutes to produce (feasible at scale)
4. It naturally leads to "want the full picture?" (assessment upsell)
5. It is valuable enough that the prospect would pay for it (Hormozi rule: free stuff better than competitors' paid stuff)

### 4.3 Sales Funnel: Lead to Close

**Stage 1: Awareness**
- Trigger: Cold outreach with a specific data point, or content that introduces the concept
- CTA: "Want your free AI Employer Visibility Snapshot?"
- Conversion target: 15-20% reply rate on cold outreach, 5-8% conversion to snapshot request

**Stage 2: Snapshot Delivery**
- Deliver the 1-page snapshot (automated or semi-automated)
- Follow up with: "Three things stood out in your results. Worth a 20-minute walkthrough?"
- Conversion target: 40-50% of snapshot recipients take the call

**Stage 3: Discovery Call (20 minutes)**
- Walk through the snapshot findings
- Show the live dashboard (the actual product)
- Diagnose: which competitor is winning, which queries matter most, what is the cost of inaction
- CTA: "The full assessment covers 50+ queries, all your competitors, citation gaps, and a prioritized remediation plan. Here is what it costs."
- Conversion target: 30-40% move to proposal

**Stage 4: Assessment Proposal**
- Deliver scope, timeline (2-3 weeks), pricing
- Include the guarantee: "If we don't find at least 3 visibility gaps you didn't know about, it's free."
- Conversion target: 50-60% close rate (high because the snapshot already proved value)

**Stage 5: Assessment Delivery and Expand**
- Deliver the full executive audit report
- Present findings in a 45-minute executive briefing
- CTA: "Want to track this quarterly? Here is the ongoing monitoring plan."
- Conversion target: 40-50% convert to ongoing engagement

### 4.4 Referral/Affiliate Play for Enterprise

Enterprise does not do formal affiliate programs. But enterprise does have:

**The "Board Packet" play:**
When a CPO or VP TA receives the audit report, they share it with their board or leadership team. The report is formatted for executive presentation. This is built into the product (the export page).

Make the report so good that the buyer's peers ask: "Who did this?"

Specific tactics:
- Add a small "Prepared by Antellion" footer (already in the export -- confirmed)
- Offer to run a complimentary snapshot for any company the buyer recommends
- Ask every satisfied client: "Who are the 2-3 TA leaders in your network who would find this valuable?"
- Create a simple referral incentive: "Refer a company that completes an assessment, get a free quarterly re-scan."

**The "Peer Network" play:**
Target TA leader communities: talent acquisition Slack groups, SHRM chapters, ERE conferences, HR Transform attendees. Not as a vendor -- as the person who introduces the concept of AI employer visibility.

### 4.5 Content Strategy for Category Creation

The content strategy has one job: make "AI employer visibility" a concept buyers recognize and care about.

**Content Pillar 1: "The AI Employer Report"** (monthly)
- Pick 5 well-known employers in one industry
- Run them through the visibility scan methodology
- Publish the comparison: "AI Employer Visibility: Big 4 Consulting" or "AI Employer Visibility: FAANG Engineering"
- This is the content that gets shared, linked, and referenced

**Content Pillar 2: "What AI Says About..." series** (weekly)
- Short, specific posts: "We asked ChatGPT who the best employers for data scientists are. Here is what it said."
- Screenshots of actual AI responses (real, not fabricated)
- Tag the companies mentioned. Some will respond. That is the engagement loop.

**Content Pillar 3: "The AI Visibility Gap"** (bi-weekly)
- Educational content: "Why your employer brand strategy has a blind spot"
- "3 things AI gets wrong about your company (and how to fix them)"
- "The new employer brand surface: AI answers"
- Position Antellion's methodology as the way to think about this

**Content Pillar 4: Executive-level thought leadership** (monthly)
- "What the CPO needs to know about AI candidate discovery"
- "Board-level question: do we control our AI employer narrative?"
- LinkedIn articles and posts targeting the buyer persona

**Distribution:** LinkedIn first (where TA leaders live). Then HR media (ERE, TLNT, HR Brew). Then broader business media once the category has traction.

## 5. PRICING ARCHITECTURE FOR HYPERSCALE

### 5.1 Value-Based Pricing Model

Antellion's value scales directly with the size of the employer. A company hiring 500 people per year gets 500x more value from improved AI visibility than a company hiring 1 person. Price accordingly.

**Three Tiers:**

---

**Tier 1: "Visibility Assessment"**
*For companies that need to understand their current AI employer position.*

- One-time AI employer visibility audit
- 1 client company + up to 3 competitors
- 30-50 candidate-intent queries across target roles
- Full scan with visibility, sentiment, and citation analysis
- Executive audit report with prioritized recommendations
- Export-ready document for leadership presentation

**Pricing:**
- Mid-market ($50M-$500M revenue): $7,500
- Enterprise ($500M+): $15,000
- Enterprise with multiple business units/brands: $25,000

---

**Tier 2: "Visibility Intelligence"**
*For companies that want ongoing monitoring and competitive tracking.*

- Everything in the Assessment, plus:
- Quarterly re-scans (4x/year)
- Up to 5 competitors tracked continuously
- Query cluster expansion as new roles open
- Quarterly executive briefing with trend analysis
- Citation gap monitoring with alerts
- Content asset tracking and recommendations

**Pricing (annual):**
- Mid-market: $30,000/year ($2,500/month equivalent)
- Enterprise: $60,000/year ($5,000/month equivalent)
- Multi-brand enterprise: $100,000/year

---

**Tier 3: "Strategic Visibility Program"** (the ultra-high-ticket anchor)
*For companies that want to own their AI employer narrative.*

- Everything in Intelligence, plus:
- Monthly scans (12x/year)
- Up to 10 competitors
- Custom query clusters for every open role family
- Dedicated visibility strategist (human, not just platform)

**Pricing (annual):**
- Enterprise only: $150,000-$250,000/year

This tier exists primarily as an anchor. When a buyer sees $150K-$250K, the $60K tier feels reasonable. Some will buy it -- and it is deliverable because the incremental work is mostly frequency and strategic advisory.

### 5.3 Mid-Market vs. Enterprise Pricing Structure

**Mid-market ($50M-$500M revenue):**
- Shorter sales cycle (2-4 weeks to close assessment, 4-8 weeks for ongoing)
- Buyer is usually VP TA or Head of Employer Brand
- Budget comes from TA or HR budget, not discretionary executive budget
- Price anchored to what they spend on employer brand agencies or Glassdoor Enhanced profiles ($10K-$30K/year)
- Assessment at $7,500 is positioned as "less than one month of your Glassdoor Enhanced spend, for intelligence Glassdoor cannot provide"

**Enterprise ($500M+):**
- Longer sales cycle (4-8 weeks for assessment, 8-16 weeks for ongoing)
- Multiple stakeholders: VP TA sponsors, CPO approves, procurement negotiates
- Budget comes from employer brand, talent strategy, or brand/marketing budget
- Price anchored to what they spend on employer brand consultancies ($50K-$200K/year)
- Assessment at $15,000 is positioned as "10% of what McKinsey would charge for half the specificity"

### 5.4 Annual vs. Monthly Billing

**Default to annual billing.** Enterprise procurement prefers annual contracts. Monthly billing for enterprise signals a tool, not a strategic investment.

Monthly billing option exists only for:
- Mid-market companies that need budget flexibility
- Priced at a 20% premium over annual ($3,000/month vs. $30K/year for mid-market)

This follows the Hormozi principle: less frequent billing = less churn. Annual billing also increases the "lookback window of value" -- the buyer evaluates value against the full year of scans and improvements, not last month.

### 5.5 Customer Surplus: Where Word-of-Mouth Lives

The customer surplus (difference between what they pay and the value they perceive) must be massive at the Assessment level to drive word-of-mouth.

**How to create surplus at $7,500-$15,000:**
- The audit report is formatted for board presentation. When the buyer walks into a leadership meeting with a branded, data-rich AI visibility report, the perceived value is "consulting-firm quality" ($50K+ in their mind).
- The competitor comparison data is something no one else provides. When a VP TA can say "We trail Deloitte by 42 percentage points in AI visibility for consultant roles," that data point alone justifies the spend.
- The citation gap playbook is immediately actionable. When the buyer's team can go act on it the next day, the value feels disproportionate to the cost.

**Surplus target:** The buyer should feel like they got $50K+ of value for $7,500-$15,000. This is what drives the "you need to talk to these people" referral.

## 6. REVENUE HYPERGROWTH ROADMAP

### Phase 1: $0 to $1M ARR (Months 1-12)

**Goal:** Prove the sales motion, establish the category narrative, close 30-40 clients.

**Revenue math:**
- 20 Assessment-only clients at avg. $10K = $200K (one-time)
- 15 Intelligence clients at avg. $45K/year = $675K ARR
- Total: $200K one-time + $675K recurring = $875K year-1 revenue
- To hit $1M ARR: need ~22 Intelligence clients or equivalent mix

**What to do:**

*Sales (70% of time):*
- Founder-led sales. No SDRs yet. Every call, the founder runs.
- 50 personalized cold outreach sequences per week
- Lead with the Visibility Snapshot (free, 10-query scan)
- Convert snapshots to paid Assessments
- Convert Assessments to Intelligence subscriptions
- Target industries where hiring competition is acute: technology, consulting, financial services, healthcare
- Target company size: $100M-$2B revenue (big enough to care, small enough to decide fast)

*Product (20% of time):*
- Keep the scan process semi-manual but efficient
- Improve report quality with every delivery
- Build seed data for 2-3 demo accounts (realistic companies, real competitive landscapes)
- Prioritize: report export polish, content asset tracking, query cluster editing

*Content (10% of time):*
- Publish the first "AI Employer Visibility Report" (pick Big 4 Consulting or FAANG)
- Start the LinkedIn content cadence (3x/week)
- Build the category vocabulary: AI employer visibility, AI mention rate, citation gap, visibility score

**Key metrics:**
- Outreach reply rate (target: 15-20%)
- Snapshot-to-call conversion (target: 40-50%)
- Assessment close rate (target: 50-60%)
- Assessment-to-Intelligence conversion (target: 40-50%)
- Time to deliver assessment (target: <3 weeks)
- NPS or qualitative feedback from first 10 clients

**What breaks:**
- Founder capacity on sales + delivery simultaneously. Mitigate: hire a technical delivery person by client 10 to run scans and produce reports.
- Scan process takes too long per client. Mitigate: identify the most time-consuming manual steps and build automation for those first. Do not try to automate everything at once.
- Buyers do not understand the category. Mitigate: lead with the data, not the concept. Show them their score first, explain the category second.
