# Master Services Agreement — Template

> **⚠️ NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is a structural starting point based on common B2B consulting MSA patterns. **Have it reviewed by a licensed attorney before sending it to any customer.** Some jurisdictions, industries, and enterprise procurement teams will require material changes. The indemnification, limitation of liability, insurance, and IP clauses in particular benefit from counsel review before first use.

---

## How to use this document

- This file is the **source template**. Keep it versioned in the repo.
- For each actual engagement, **export to PDF or Word**, replace bracketed placeholders (`[CUSTOMER NAME]`, `[EFFECTIVE DATE]`, etc.), and strip all **Drafter's Note** blocks before sending.
- The MSA governs the **relationship**. Each engagement's specific scope, deliverables, and fees live in a separate **Statement of Work (SOW)** that references this MSA.
- Bracketed fields are intentionally explicit — search for `[` to find every field requiring input.
- Customer may request redlines. Track them deal-by-deal; if the same redline appears three times, update the template.

# MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Services Agreement (this "**Agreement**") is entered into as of **[EFFECTIVE DATE]** (the "**Effective Date**") by and between:

**Antellion [LLC / Inc.]**, a **[STATE OF FORMATION]** **[entity type]** with a principal place of business at **[ANTELLION ADDRESS]** ("**Antellion**"), and

**[CUSTOMER LEGAL NAME]**, a **[CUSTOMER ENTITY TYPE AND STATE]** with a principal place of business at **[CUSTOMER ADDRESS]** ("**Customer**").

Antellion and Customer are each a "**Party**" and collectively the "**Parties**."

> **Drafter's Note:** Confirm Antellion's legal entity name and formation state before use. If Antellion is operating as a sole proprietorship rather than LLC/corp, the liability exposure is materially different and counsel review is especially important.

## 1. Purpose and Structure

### 1.1 Purpose

Antellion provides AI employer visibility assessment services that measure how artificial intelligence models (including ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and similar systems) describe employers when candidates ask about employment opportunities. Services are delivered as human-interpreted written analyses, remediation recommendations, and executive advisory sessions.

### 1.2 Structure of the Relationship

This Agreement establishes the general terms governing all services Antellion provides to Customer. Specific engagements — including scope, deliverables, fees, and schedules — are set forth in separate **Statements of Work** ("**SOW**") that each reference and incorporate this Agreement by reference.

### 1.3 Order of Precedence

In the event of a conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, the SOW controls **only to the extent expressly stated in the SOW**. Otherwise, this Agreement controls.

---

## 2. Services

### 2.1 Services

Antellion will perform the services described in each executed SOW (the "**Services**"). Services may include, without limitation:

- AI employer visibility assessments (Baseline, Focused, Strategic, or Enterprise scope)
- Written reports with remediation recommendations
- Executive readout presentations
- Quarterly re-scans and ongoing measurement
- Advisory sessions and strategic guidance

### 2.2 Two-Phase Engagement Structure

Engagements may be structured in phases, as specified in each SOW:

- **Phase 1 — Baseline Audit:** An initial foundational assessment that establishes the fact pattern for further work.
- **Phase 2 — Extended Audit:** Optional extended work (Focused, Strategic, or Enterprise scope) priced against Phase 1 findings.

**Default structure — separate SOWs per phase.** By default, Phase 1 and Phase 2 are engaged under separate SOWs. Customer signs an SOW for Phase 1 Baseline Audit, pays the Phase 1 deposit, and Antellion delivers Phase 1. After Phase 1 delivery and the associated readout, if Customer elects to proceed to Phase 2, a separate SOW is executed covering Phase 2 scope and fees. Under this default, **no Phase 2 fees are invoiced or collected before Phase 1 is complete**, and Customer's decision whether to proceed to Phase 2 is made with the benefit of Phase 1 findings in hand.

**Combined SOW (exception).** Where Customer's procurement process requires a single contract covering both phases, the SOW may cover Phase 1 and Phase 2 together with a single engagement fee. In that case:

- The deposit (fifty percent (50%) of the combined engagement fee) is payable upon SOW execution;
- **Customer remains not obligated to proceed to Phase 2** upon Phase 1 delivery; and
- If Customer elects not to proceed to Phase 2 within thirty (30) days of Phase 1 delivery, Antellion will refund the Phase 2 portion of any deposit paid within thirty (30) days of Customer's written notice, and the engagement will be deemed complete upon Phase 1 delivery.

> **Drafter's Note:** This section operationalizes the "never obligated to proceed" guarantee. Model A (separate SOWs) is the default and should be used whenever Customer's procurement allows. Model B (combined SOW with refund clause) should only be used when Customer's procurement process requires a single contract. The Phase 2 portion of any deposit is always refundable if Customer opts out after Phase 1.

### 2.3 Customer Cooperation

Customer agrees to provide, in a timely manner, the information reasonably necessary for Antellion to perform Services, including but not limited to: company identifying information, named competitors, job categories in scope, and access to any Customer personnel required for readout sessions.

## 3. Fees, Payment, and Taxes

### 3.1 Fees

Fees for each engagement are specified in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise stated in the SOW, all fees are in U.S. Dollars and exclusive of applicable taxes.

### 3.2 Payment Terms

Unless otherwise stated in the SOW:

- A deposit equal to **fifty percent (50%)** of the total engagement fee is due within **fifteen (15) days** of SOW signature or quote acceptance.
- The remaining **fifty percent (50%)** balance is due within **thirty (30) days** of Antellion's delivery of the engagement's final deliverable.
- Work on Phase 1 will commence upon Antellion's receipt of the deposit.

### 3.3 Invoicing and Payment Methods

Antellion will issue invoices through Stripe. Customer may pay by ACH bank transfer, wire transfer, or credit card. Credit card payments of over **$25,000** are subject to a **three percent (3%)** processing surcharge.

### 3.4 Late Payments

Invoices unpaid **fifteen (15) days** past their due date accrue interest at **one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month** (or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower). Antellion reserves the right to pause delivery of Services on engagements with past-due invoices.

### 3.5 Taxes

Customer is responsible for all sales, use, value-added, and similar taxes arising out of the Services, excluding taxes based on Antellion's net income.

## 4. Term and Termination

### 4.1 Term

This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated in accordance with Section 4.2 or Section 4.3 (the "**Term**").

### 4.2 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing **thirty (30) days' written notice** to the other Party, provided that active SOWs will continue under their existing terms unless separately terminated.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any active SOW immediately upon written notice if the other Party:

- Materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within **thirty (30) days** of written notice describing the breach; or
- Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or ceases to operate as a going concern.

### 4.4 Effect of Termination

Upon termination of this Agreement or any SOW:

- Customer will pay Antellion for all Services performed and deliverables provided up to the effective date of termination, on a pro-rata basis;
- Antellion will deliver any in-progress work product in its then-current state; and
- Each Party's obligations that by their nature survive termination (including confidentiality, IP, warranty disclaimers, limitation of liability, and indemnification) will survive.

## 5. Confidentiality

### 5.1 Definition

"**Confidential Information**" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party ("**Disclosing Party**") to the other ("**Receiving Party**") that is marked or identified as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential under the circumstances. Confidential Information includes, without limitation: business strategies, customer data, unpublished financial information, competitive analyses, and proprietary methodologies.

### 5.2 Obligations

Each Party agrees to:

- Use the other Party's Confidential Information solely to perform its obligations under this Agreement;
- Protect such Confidential Information using the same standard of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care; and
- Not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party except to its employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement.

### 5.3 Exceptions

The obligations in Section 5.2 do not apply to information that:

- Is or becomes publicly known through no fault of the Receiving Party;
- Was lawfully known to the Receiving Party before receipt from the Disclosing Party;
- Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or
- Is required to be disclosed by law, provided the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt notice and cooperates with any effort to limit or contest the disclosure.

### 5.4 Duration

The obligations in this Section 5 survive termination of this Agreement for **three (3) years**, except that Confidential Information constituting a trade secret remains protected for as long as it qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law.

## 6. Data Protection

### 6.1 Data Collected

Antellion collects and processes minimal Customer information to perform Services, including: Customer's company name, domain, named competitors, job categories in scope, and industry context. Antellion does not collect Customer's employee personal data, applicant data, or HR records as part of Services.

### 6.2 Data Handling

Antellion will:

- Store Customer information on encrypted infrastructure with industry-standard access controls;
- Limit access to Customer information to personnel who require such access to perform Services;
- Not sell, share, or distribute Customer information to third parties, except to subcontractors engaged to perform Services who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement.

### 6.3 AI Model Interactions

As part of Services, Antellion submits queries to third-party AI systems to analyze how those systems describe Customer and Customer's competitors to prospective candidates. The AI systems currently used include OpenAI's ChatGPT, Anthropic's Claude, and Google's Gemini, and may include additional AI systems (such as Perplexity, Microsoft Copilot, or successor systems) as Antellion's methodology evolves or as Customer specifically requests in an SOW.

**Data submitted to AI systems.** Queries submitted to these AI systems contain only:

- Customer's company name;
- Customer's domain or brand name references necessary for clear identification;
- Named competitor companies specified in the SOW;
- Industry descriptors and job category names in scope;
- Query text formatted as candidate-intent questions (for example, "Best companies to work for in [industry]?" or "What is it like to work at [Customer Name]?").

**Data not submitted to AI systems.** Queries do not contain Customer's employee personal data, applicant records, internal HR data, proprietary EVP or employer brand materials, financial data, salary data, or any Confidential Information specific to Customer's internal operations. The query data submitted is limited to information that is either publicly available or inferable from Customer's public identity.

**Third-party processing.** Customer acknowledges that:

- These AI systems are operated by third parties with their own data handling policies;
- Antellion does not control how these third parties process query data; and
- Antellion will make commercially reasonable efforts to configure API interactions to minimize data retention by third-party AI providers where such configuration is available (for example, using enterprise API tiers that opt out of model training where offered).

> **Drafter's Note:** This section is load-bearing for enterprise security reviews. The "Data not submitted" paragraph is the key reassurance — update it as the pipeline evolves if any additional data categories start being transmitted. When Antellion adopts enterprise API tiers that offer no-training / zero-retention configurations (OpenAI Enterprise, Anthropic Enterprise, Google Cloud Vertex), update the "commercially reasonable efforts" language to name specific configurations. Update the "AI systems currently used" list when new models are added to the production pipeline.

### 6.4 Data Processing Agreement

If Customer requires a separate Data Processing Agreement (DPA), Antellion will execute Customer's standard DPA in substantially its proposed form, subject to reasonable Antellion review.

## 7. Intellectual Property

### 7.1 Customer Deliverables

Upon Customer's payment in full of all fees owed for an engagement, Antellion grants Customer a **perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive license** to use, copy, and distribute internally the final deliverables produced specifically for Customer (reports, remediation recommendations, executive readout materials) for Customer's internal business purposes.

### 7.2 Antellion Retained IP

Antellion retains all right, title, and interest in and to its **Methodology**, including without limitation: query templates and frameworks, scoring methodologies, citation ecosystem taxonomy, candidate decision journey framework, competitive analysis procedures, software tools, and any other intellectual property developed by or for Antellion independent of or alongside its work for Customer.

### 7.3 Aggregated and Anonymized Data

Antellion may use anonymized, aggregated insights derived from Services performed across multiple customers for the purpose of improving its Methodology, conducting research, and publishing industry benchmarks, provided that no such use identifies Customer, Customer's competitors by name in a manner specific to Customer's engagement, or any Confidential Information specific to Customer.

### 7.4 Feedback

Any feedback, suggestions, or ideas Customer provides regarding Antellion's Methodology or Services may be used by Antellion without restriction or obligation.

## 8. Warranties and Disclaimers

### 8.1 Mutual Warranties

Each Party warrants that:

- It has the full authority to enter into this Agreement; and
- Its performance under this Agreement will not violate any applicable law or any agreement to which it is a party.

### 8.2 Antellion Service Warranties

Antellion warrants that:

- Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with generally accepted industry standards;
- Deliverables will meet the specific completeness criteria stated in the applicable SOW (including minimum findings counts and minimum remediation actions where specified); and
- Deliverables will be delivered within the timelines stated in the applicable SOW, subject to Customer's timely cooperation per Section 2.3.

### 8.3 Remedy for Breach of Service Warranty

If Antellion breaches the warranties in Section 8.2, Customer's sole and exclusive remedies are:

- For failure to meet deliverable completeness criteria: **full refund of fees paid for the affected engagement**; and
- For factual errors identified in the deliverable: Antellion will correct the error within **48 hours** of written notice, or refund fees paid for the affected engagement.

### 8.4 Disclaimer of AI Output Warranties

**Customer acknowledges and agrees** that:

- Antellion does not control the outputs of third-party AI systems;
- AI system outputs change over time and vary by query, context, and model updates;
- Antellion's analyses reflect observed AI outputs at a specific point in time and are not a guarantee of future AI behavior;
- Antellion does not warrant that implementing remediation recommendations will produce specific business outcomes, hiring outcomes, or changes in AI model behavior.

### 8.5 Disclaimer

EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY PROVIDED IN THIS AGREEMENT, ANTELLION MAKES NO OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, AND SPECIFICALLY DISCLAIMS ALL IMPLIED WARRANTIES, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT.

> **Drafter's Note:** Section 8.3 is load-bearing — it operationalizes the "Antellion-controlled risk reversal" principle from the offer stack. The remedy is tied to what Antellion ships (completeness, accuracy), never to downstream business outcomes. Section 8.4 disclaims warranties about what AI actually says (outside Antellion's control). Counsel will likely strengthen this language — keep the substance.

## 9. Limitation of Liability

### 9.1 Cap on Damages

**EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF SECTION 5 (CONFIDENTIALITY), SECTION 7 (INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY), OR A PARTY'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS,** EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY TO THE OTHER PARTY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT WILL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER TO ANTELLION IN THE **TWELVE (12) MONTHS** PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

### 9.2 Exclusion of Certain Damages

IN NO EVENT WILL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, LOST BUSINESS OPPORTUNITY, OR COSTS OF COVER, REGARDLESS OF THE FORM OF ACTION.

> **Drafter's Note:** Enterprise customers (Wells Fargo, Avis, etc.) often push back on liability caps limited to 12 months of fees. Common compromises: 2x annual fees, or a floor like "$1M or fees paid, whichever is greater." Counsel should review and advise on what's negotiable. For solo-founder risk exposure, the 12-month cap in 9.1 is protective — do not surrender it without legal advice.

## 10. Indemnification

### 10.1 Antellion Indemnification

Antellion will defend and indemnify Customer against any third-party claim alleging that the Services as delivered by Antellion infringe a third party's patent, copyright, or trademark. Antellion has no obligation under this Section 10.1 to the extent a claim arises from: (a) Customer's modification of a deliverable; (b) combination of a deliverable with materials not provided by Antellion; or (c) Customer's use of a deliverable outside the scope of the license granted in Section 7.1.

### 10.2 Customer Indemnification

Customer will defend and indemnify Antellion against any third-party claim arising from: (a) Customer's misuse of a deliverable; (b) Customer's breach of Section 5 (Confidentiality); or (c) Customer's violation of applicable law in its use of Services or deliverables.

### 10.3 Indemnification Procedure

The indemnified Party must: (i) promptly notify the indemnifying Party of the claim; (ii) grant the indemnifying Party sole control of the defense and settlement; and (iii) cooperate reasonably at the indemnifying Party's expense.

## 11. Insurance

Antellion will maintain the following insurance coverage during the Term:

- **General Liability Insurance:** At least **$1,000,000** per occurrence;
- **Professional Liability / Errors and Omissions Insurance:** At least **$1,000,000** per claim; and
- **Cyber Liability Insurance:** At least **$1,000,000** per claim.

Antellion will provide Customer with certificates of insurance evidencing such coverage upon reasonable request.

> **Drafter's Note:** These coverage levels are common for mid-market B2B consulting MSAs. Verify Antellion actually has (or can get) these policies before signing an MSA with them in it. Typical cost for a solo-founder consulting business: $1,500-$3,000/year combined premium. Enterprise customers will often specify higher limits ($2M or $5M); push back politely or upgrade policies as required by specific deals.

## 12. Force Majeure

Neither Party will be liable for any delay or failure to perform under this Agreement due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including without limitation: acts of God, natural disasters, pandemics, acts of war or terrorism, government actions, or failures of public utilities or the internet. The affected Party will give prompt notice of the force majeure event and use reasonable efforts to resume performance.

## 13. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

### 13.1 Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the **[STATE — e.g., Delaware, or Antellion's state of formation]**, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

### 13.2 Dispute Resolution

The Parties will first attempt to resolve any dispute arising out of or related to this Agreement through good-faith negotiation between senior executives of each Party. If the dispute is not resolved within **thirty (30) days** of either Party's written notice of the dispute, the dispute will be submitted to binding arbitration in **[CITY, STATE]** under the Commercial Arbitration Rules of the American Arbitration Association, with the decision of the arbitrator enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### 13.3 Equitable Relief

Notwithstanding Section 13.2, either Party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to enforce Section 5 (Confidentiality), Section 7 (Intellectual Property), or to prevent immediate and irreparable harm.

> **Drafter's Note:** Governing law and venue are commonly negotiated. Enterprise customers typically prefer their own state; Antellion may accept that for strategic accounts. Arbitration vs. court litigation is a meaningful choice — arbitration is faster and confidential, but limits appeal rights. Counsel should advise based on Antellion's risk profile.

# Appendix: Notes for Counsel Review

Before using this template in live deals, a licensed attorney should review:

1. **Entity formation basis.** Is Antellion an LLC, S-corp, C-corp, or sole proprietorship? Liability exposure differs materially.
2. **Section 2.2 (Phase 2 contingency).** Verify the refund-on-opt-out language doesn't inadvertently create obligations Antellion can't meet.
3. **Section 6.3 (AI model interactions).** Has Antellion reviewed the third-party API provider terms (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google)? Does Antellion pass customer identifiers in ways that need explicit disclosure or consent?
4. **Section 7.3 (aggregated data).** Is the language broad enough to support future product features (industry benchmarks, etc.) without risking enterprise customer objection?
5. **Section 8.3 (warranty remedy).** The "full refund" remedy is strong. Counsel should verify it doesn't conflict with other jurisdictional requirements for warranty limitations.
6. **Section 9.1 (liability cap).** The 12-month fee cap is founder-protective. Enterprise customers will negotiate. Decide a minimum acceptable floor before negotiations start.
7. **Section 11 (insurance).** Does Antellion currently carry these policies? If not, obtain quotes and secure coverage before first use.
8. **Section 13 (governing law).** State-specific. Delaware is a common default but may not be optimal depending on Antellion's actual operations.
9. **Industry-specific regulations.** If Antellion sells to regulated industries (healthcare, financial services, government), sector-specific clauses (HIPAA, GLBA, FedRAMP, etc.) may be required.
10. **DPA template.** Section 6.4 references customer DPAs. Antellion should have its own DPA template ready rather than always accepting the customer's.
