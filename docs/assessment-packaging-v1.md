### Proposal / SOW (1 page)

**Scope of Work: AI Employer Visibility Assessment**

**Objective.** Produce a comprehensive, evidence-backed assessment of how [Client] appears in AI-generated responses when candidates research employers in the [Industry] sector. The assessment evaluates [Client] against [N] named competitors across 120+ candidate-intent queries, mapped to four stages of the candidate decision journey.

**Methodology.** Antellion operates a structured, seven-layer assessment framework:

**Layer 1: Query Intelligence.** We design 120+ candidate-intent queries across six themes -- Reputation & Brand, Compensation & Benefits, Culture & Work-Life, Role Expectations, Hiring Process, and Competitor Comparison -- covering every meaningful combination of decision stage, intent theme, role variant, and competitive pairing. Each query maps to a specific stage of the candidate decision journey (Discovery, Consideration, Evaluation, Commitment) and models a real question candidates ask AI when evaluating employers. The result is a query set dense enough to surface per-stage, per-theme, and per-competitor patterns that smaller samples miss entirely.

**Layer 2: Visibility Scanning.** Each query is executed against AI models, and the full response is captured with provenance metadata: query text, model, parameters, timestamp, and raw response. This is the evidence chain that makes every subsequent claim auditable.

**Layer 3: Deterministic Analysis.** Each response is scored using a structured analysis pipeline -- not a subjective read. The system detects entity mentions (client and each competitor) using word-boundary matching, scores visibility (0-100) based on mention frequency, position, and signal context, scores sentiment (-1 to 1) from positive/negative signal ratios, and extracts all cited domains.

**Layer 4: Decision Journey Classification.** Every query-response pair is classified into the appropriate decision stage using rule-based classifiers that evaluate whether the query references the client, contains comparison terms, or includes commitment-intent language (interview, hiring process, application). This classification powers the per-stage analysis.

**Layer 5: Competitive Comparison.** Cross-entity analysis computes per-entity mention rates, competitive gap percentages, citation gap domains (platforms AI cites for competitors but not the client), and per-stage displacement patterns showing which competitor dominates at which decision stage.

**Layer 6: Confidence Scoring & Anti-Overclaim Penalties.** Every aggregated finding is scored on four confidence factors: sample size, signal consistency, citation coverage, and response quality. Anti-overclaim penalties are applied when the sample is small, the scan is single-run, completion is below threshold, or the mention rate falls near 50/50 on a small sample. Report language adjusts automatically -- high-confidence data is asserted directly; low-confidence data is hedged transparently.

**Layer 7: QA Verification.** Before delivery, the report passes 12 automated quality checks across five categories: Completeness (scans completed, results present, summary exists), Evidence Integrity (all results approved, all have responses, confidence acceptable), Source Accuracy (citations present for mentions, no empty domains), Narrative (quantitative data incorporated, sections present), and Competitor Logic (all competitors represented, mention data exists). Blocking checks prevent delivery of incomplete reports.

**Deliverables.**
- Executive audit report, print-ready (HTML export, A4 formatted)
- Cover page with client branding and confidentiality designation
- 7-section structured report: Executive Summary, Candidate Decision Journey (with funnel diagram), Competitive Landscape (with displacement matrix), Citation Ecosystem (with platform gap table), Narrative Positioning (with per-stage positioning classification), Pipeline Impact (with compounded funnel throughput), and Prioritized Remediation Plan (with per-recommendation evidence basis, actions, effort, and timeframe)
- 30-minute walkthrough of findings with Q&A
- Digital delivery of all findings

**Timeline.** Assessment delivered within 10 business days of engagement start.

## Part 2: Why This Is Not DIY

### The "I could do this myself with ChatGPT" objection -- answered specifically

**You could ask ChatGPT 10 questions about your company.** We design 120+ queries across six candidate-intent themes (Reputation, Compensation, Culture, Role Expectations, Hiring Process, Competitor Comparison), each mapped to a specific stage of the candidate decision journey. The query set covers every meaningful combination of decision stage, intent theme, role variant, geographic scope, and competitive pairing -- from "best companies for [role] in [city]" (Discovery) through "[Company] vs [Competitor] for [role]" (Evaluation) to "how to get hired at [Company]" (Commitment). 120 queries is the threshold where per-stage and per-theme patterns become statistically distinguishable from noise. You would need to design this taxonomy yourself, which requires understanding both candidate behavior, AI prompt design, and query-matrix methodology.

**You could read the AI responses and form an opinion.** We score every response using a deterministic analysis pipeline: word-boundary mention detection for your company and every competitor, visibility scoring (0-100) based on mention frequency, mention position, and contextual signal words, sentiment scoring (-1 to 1) from positive/negative keyword ratios, and citation domain extraction. This is not "I read it and it seemed positive." This is structured, repeatable measurement that produces comparable numbers across companies, time periods, and AI models.

**You could compare yourself with one competitor.** We map competitive displacement across four decision stages and compute per-stage mention rates, per-stage positioning tiers (Champion/Contender/Peripheral/Cautionary/Invisible), and per-stage gap percentages for every competitor. The system identifies which specific competitor displaces you at which stage -- you might be strong at Discovery but invisible at Evaluation, while a competitor you do not consider a threat dominates the comparison stage. With 120+ scored query-response pairs, the cross-entity, cross-stage analysis reaches the density required to distinguish real patterns from noise at the per-theme, per-competitor level -- something a 10-query spot check cannot do.

**You could note which platforms AI mentions.** We perform citation ecosystem mapping that separates domains into three categories: client-exclusive domains (platforms that appear when AI mentions you), gap domains (platforms AI cites for competitors but not for you), and shared domains. Then we cross-reference gap domains against decision stages to show which platforms drive the gap at which stage. Platform knowledge built into the system maps domains like levels.fyi, glassdoor.com, blind.app, and builtin.com to their stage relevance, content type, and recommended actions. This is not a list of URLs -- it is a strategic map of which platforms to prioritize and why.

**You could write up your findings in a document.** We compose a 7-section executive report with confidence-aware language that automatically adjusts claims based on statistical strength (high confidence = direct assertion, medium = scoped to assessed data, low = transparently acknowledges sample limitations). The report includes a compounded funnel throughput calculation showing what fraction of AI-researching candidates survive all four journey stages. It includes per-recommendation evidence basis statements that tie each action to the specific scan data that triggered it. It passes 12 automated QA checks before delivery. The report is designed for executive presentation -- cover page, print-ready formatting, confidentiality designation.

**You could do parts of this in a weekend. You could not do all of it.** The system we built to produce this assessment includes: a query-matrix generator that produces 120+ queries across every stage-theme-competitor combination, a stage classifier, a positioning classifier (with five tiers based on mention rate, visibility, and sentiment thresholds), a funnel throughput calculator, a recommendation engine with stage-specific logic and platform knowledge for 9+ employer platforms, a confidence scoring system with four weighted factors and four anti-overclaim penalty rules, an evidence immutability layer that locks 16 provenance fields once data is approved (so findings cannot be retroactively altered), and a 12-check QA system that gates report delivery. Executing, scoring, and cross-referencing 120+ queries against 4-8 competitors across 4 stages and 6 themes is not something you assemble in a spreadsheet. It is a structured analytical methodology encoded as software.

## Appendix: What This Document Does NOT Claim

The following capabilities are NOT yet implemented and should NOT be referenced in sales conversations:

1. **Multi-model scanning** -- The data model supports multiple AI providers but current assessments use a single model. Do not claim "we scan across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude." When multi-model is implemented, it becomes a Premium/Enterprise differentiator.

2. **Automated longitudinal comparison** -- The system supports multiple scan runs per client, but there is no automated trend computation. Quarterly comparisons are currently narrative (manual comparison of current vs. baseline findings). The automated trend feature is on the roadmap.

3. **Continuous monitoring** -- Assessments are point-in-time. There is no real-time dashboard or alert system. Do not claim "we monitor your visibility continuously."

4. **AI-powered recommendations** -- Recommendations are deterministic and template-driven, which is a strength (consistent, auditable, no hallucination). Do not accidentally call them "AI-generated."

5. **Proven improvement metrics** -- No longitudinal data exists yet. Do not claim "clients who follow our recommendations see X% improvement." Once 2-3 clients complete baseline + follow-up assessments, this proof point can be added.

6. **Client-facing portal** -- Clients receive a report, not a login. There is no self-serve access. Do not promise a client dashboard.
