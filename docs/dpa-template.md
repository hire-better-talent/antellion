# Data Processing Agreement — Template

> **⚠️ NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This template is a structural starting point for a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) designed to accompany the Antellion MSA. **Have it reviewed by a licensed attorney before sending it to any customer.** Data protection law varies by jurisdiction (GDPR in the EU/UK, CCPA/CPRA in California, state laws in the US, PIPEDA in Canada). If a customer operates in a regulated industry (healthcare, financial services, government), sector-specific requirements (HIPAA, GLBA) may require material additions. The security, sub-processor, international transfer, and audit clauses in particular benefit from counsel review before first use.

---

## How to use this template

- This file is the **source template**. Keep it versioned in the repo.
- For each customer that requires a separate DPA, **export to PDF**, replace bracketed placeholders, and strip all **Drafter's Note** blocks before sending.
- The DPA is signed **alongside or after** the MSA. It does not replace the MSA — it supplements it for data protection specifics.
- If the customer proposes their own DPA instead, MSA Section 6.4 allows Antellion to execute the customer's DPA "in substantially its proposed form, subject to reasonable Antellion review." Use this template as a **reference** to evaluate whether the customer's DPA is materially worse than Antellion's standard.
- Antellion's actual data processing scope is narrow. The template reflects that honestly — do not pad it with processing claims that do not apply.

# DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

This Data Processing Agreement (the "**DPA**") is entered into as of **[EFFECTIVE DATE]** (the "**Effective Date**") and is incorporated into and forms part of the Master Services Agreement between **Hire Better Talent LLC, doing business as "Antellion"** ("**Processor**" or "**Antellion**") and **[CUSTOMER LEGAL NAME]** ("**Controller**" or "**Customer**") dated **[MSA EFFECTIVE DATE]** (the "**MSA**").

In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the MSA, this DPA controls with respect to data protection matters only.

---

## 1. Definitions

The following terms have the meanings set forth below. Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings given to them in the MSA or applicable Data Protection Laws.

- **"Data Protection Laws"** means all laws and regulations applicable to the Parties' processing of Personal Data under this DPA, including without limitation: (i) the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679, "**GDPR**"); (ii) the UK GDPR and UK Data Protection Act 2018; (iii) the California Consumer Privacy Act (as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act, "**CCPA/CPRA**"); (iv) other U.S. state privacy laws as applicable; (v) the Canadian Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (**PIPEDA**); and (vi) any successor or similar data protection laws.

- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that is processed by Processor on behalf of Controller in connection with the Services.

- **"Processing"** (and "**Process**") has the meaning given in applicable Data Protection Laws and includes any operation performed on Personal Data.

- **"Security Incident"** means a confirmed breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to Personal Data processed by Processor.

- **"Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by Processor to Process Personal Data on Processor's behalf.

- **"Services"** has the meaning given in the MSA.

> **Drafter's Note:** Data protection terms are jurisdiction-specific. Counsel should confirm which laws apply based on Controller's location, data-subject location, and industry.

---

## 2. Subject Matter and Scope

### 2.1 Subject Matter

This DPA applies to Processor's Processing of Personal Data on behalf of Controller in connection with the Services described in the MSA and any active SOWs.

### 2.2 Nature of Processing

The nature, scope, and purposes of Processing are set forth in **Annex A** (Processing Details) of this DPA.

### 2.3 Roles of the Parties

With respect to Personal Data processed under this DPA:

- Controller is the "**data controller**" (or equivalent role under applicable Data Protection Laws); and
- Processor is the "**data processor**" (or equivalent role).

The Parties acknowledge that Processor's role with respect to Personal Data is limited. The Services provided by Antellion primarily involve analysis of how third-party artificial intelligence systems describe Controller (as a company) and Controller's named competitors (as companies) to prospective candidates. This analysis does not require Processor to access Controller's employee records, applicant records, or HR systems. Personal Data Processed under this DPA is narrow in scope and primarily limited to Controller contact information and any personal names that appear in Controller-provided context or AI-system outputs.

> **Drafter's Note:** This paragraph is important — it narrows the DPA to Antellion's actual processing footprint. Enterprise buyers will often propose a broad DPA assuming deep data processing; this language sets expectations truthfully.

---

## 3. Processor Obligations

Processor will:

(a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Controller, including the instructions set forth in the MSA, this DPA, and any SOW;

(b) Ensure that personnel authorized to Process Personal Data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations (consistent with MSA Section 5);

(c) Implement the technical and organizational security measures described in **Annex C** (Security Measures) of this DPA;

(d) Engage Sub-processors only as permitted under Section 4 of this DPA;

(e) Assist Controller, taking into account the nature of the Processing and the information available to Processor, in fulfilling Controller's obligations under Data Protection Laws to respond to requests from data subjects;

(f) Notify Controller of any Security Incident affecting Personal Data consistent with the timing and content requirements of MSA Section 6.5 and Section 5 of this DPA;

(g) Make available to Controller all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA; and

(h) Upon expiration or termination of the Services, return or delete Personal Data as set forth in Section 8 of this DPA.

---

## 4. Sub-processors

### 4.1 General Authorization

Controller provides general authorization for Processor to engage Sub-processors in connection with the Services, including the categories of Sub-processors listed in **Annex B** (Sub-processors) of this DPA.

### 4.2 Processor Obligations Regarding Sub-processors

Processor will:

(a) Enter into a written agreement with each Sub-processor imposing data protection obligations no less protective than those in this DPA;

(b) Remain liable for each Sub-processor's acts and omissions in performing data protection obligations; and

(c) Upon Controller's written request, provide a current list of named Sub-processors.

### 4.3 Notice of New Sub-processors

Processor will use commercially reasonable efforts to provide Controller with notice of any material change to its Sub-processor list. Controller may object to a new Sub-processor within **thirty (30) days** of notice on reasonable data-protection grounds. If Controller's objection cannot be resolved, Controller may terminate the affected portion of the Services upon written notice.

---

## 5. Security Measures and Incident Notification

### 5.1 Security Measures

Processor will implement and maintain the technical and organizational security measures described in **Annex C** (Security Measures) and, to the extent applicable, MSA Section 6.2.

### 5.2 Security Incident Notification

In the event of a Security Incident affecting Personal Data, Processor will:

(a) Notify Controller without undue delay, and in any event within **seventy-two (72) hours** of Processor's confirmation of the incident, consistent with MSA Section 6.5;

(b) Include in such notification: (i) the nature of the incident and categories of data subjects and Personal Data affected, (ii) the likely consequences, (iii) the measures taken or proposed to address the incident, and (iv) the contact point for further information;

(c) Cooperate in good faith with Controller's investigation and remediation efforts at Processor's cost where the incident is caused by Processor's breach of this DPA or the MSA.

---

## 6. Data Subject Rights

### 6.1 Assistance to Controller

Processor will, taking into account the nature of the Processing, provide reasonable assistance to Controller in responding to requests from data subjects to exercise their rights under Data Protection Laws, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection.

### 6.2 Direct Requests to Processor

If Processor receives a request from a data subject directly, Processor will promptly notify Controller and will not respond to the request except as required by law or as instructed by Controller.

---

## 7. International Data Transfers

### 7.1 Transfers Outside the EU/UK

Where Processing involves the transfer of Personal Data from the European Union, European Economic Area, United Kingdom, or Switzerland to a country not deemed to provide adequate data protection, the Parties will implement an appropriate transfer mechanism under Data Protection Laws, which may include:

(a) **Standard Contractual Clauses** (EU Commission Decision 2021/914 or successor) incorporated by reference, with Controller as "data exporter" and Processor as "data importer"; and

(b) Any additional safeguards required by applicable Data Protection Laws.

### 7.2 Third-Country AI Providers

Controller acknowledges that, in the performance of the Services, Processor submits queries to third-party artificial intelligence systems operated by providers that may be located in, or may transfer data to, third countries. Processor will make commercially reasonable efforts to use enterprise API configurations that provide appropriate data protection safeguards where available.

> **Drafter's Note:** If Antellion ever serves EU, UK, or Swiss customers, this section becomes load-bearing. The Standard Contractual Clauses should be attached as an annex (Annex D) when actually used. For US-only deals, this section is mostly dormant but still belongs in the DPA.

---

## 8. Return and Destruction of Personal Data

Upon expiration or termination of the Services, or earlier upon Controller's written request, Processor will:

(a) Return to Controller all Personal Data Processed under this DPA, in a commonly used format, within **thirty (30) days** of the request; or

(b) Destroy all such Personal Data and certify destruction in writing.

Processor may retain Personal Data only to the extent required by law, for legitimate internal record-keeping of aggregated or de-identified data as permitted under MSA Section 7.3, or as strictly necessary to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

---

## 9. Audits

### 9.1 Audit Rights

Once per calendar year, and more frequently as required by Data Protection Laws or as reasonably necessary following a Security Incident, Controller may, upon reasonable prior written notice, audit Processor's compliance with this DPA. Audits will be:

(a) Limited in scope to Processor's Processing of Controller's Personal Data;

(b) Conducted during Processor's normal business hours;

(c) Performed by Controller or a qualified independent third-party auditor bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this DPA; and

(d) Conducted at Controller's cost, except where the audit reveals a material breach by Processor, in which case Processor will bear reasonable audit costs.

### 9.2 Alternative Assurance

In lieu of an on-site audit, Processor may satisfy audit requests by providing relevant compliance reports (such as SOC 2, ISO 27001, or Processor's own security documentation) that reasonably demonstrate compliance with this DPA.

> **Drafter's Note:** Antellion does not currently hold SOC 2 or ISO 27001 certification. Until those are in place, customers requesting formal certifications will need to accept Processor's own security documentation under Section 9.2. Plan to certify SOC 2 within 12-18 months of first Enterprise Audit engagement.

---

## 10. Liability

The liability limitations in MSA Section 9 apply to this DPA. For the avoidance of doubt, Processor's liability for breach of this DPA is subject to the same cap and carve-outs as Processor's liability under the MSA.

---

## 11. Term

This DPA enters into force on the Effective Date and terminates automatically upon termination or expiration of the MSA, except that provisions that by their nature should survive (including Processor obligations with respect to retained Personal Data) survive for the duration Processor retains Personal Data.

---

## 12. General Provisions

### 12.1 Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the same law as the MSA, except to the extent Data Protection Laws require otherwise.

### 12.2 Precedence

In case of conflict between this DPA and the MSA, this DPA controls with respect to data protection matters only.

### 12.3 Amendment

This DPA may be amended only by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both Parties, except that Processor may update **Annex B** (Sub-processors) in accordance with Section 4.3.

### 12.4 Electronic Signatures

This DPA may be executed electronically and in counterparts, each of which is deemed an original.

## Annex A — Processing Details

**Subject matter of the Processing:** Analysis of how third-party AI systems describe Controller and Controller's named competitors to prospective candidates.

**Duration of the Processing:** The term of the applicable SOW, plus any retention period permitted under Section 8 of this DPA.

**Nature and purpose of the Processing:** Processing consists of: (i) storing Controller's company identifying information and named competitor information; (ii) submitting candidate-intent queries to third-party AI systems that contain Controller's company name, competitor names, and job category context; (iii) analyzing AI system responses; (iv) storing analysis results; (v) delivering written reports and recommendations to Controller; and (vi) maintaining Controller contact information for communication purposes.

**Categories of Data Subjects:** 

- Controller's designated contacts (name, business email, business phone, business title)
- Natural persons whose names appear in AI system responses about Controller or its competitors (typically drawn from public review sites, press coverage, and community platforms; limited and incidental)

**Categories of Personal Data:**

- Business contact information (name, business email, business phone, business title) of Controller's designated contacts
- Names and role titles of natural persons that appear incidentally in third-party AI system responses analyzed as part of the Services
- No special categories of Personal Data are intentionally Processed

**Personal Data NOT Processed:**

Processor does not Process Controller's employee personal records, applicant records, compensation records, performance records, HR data, health data, or other Personal Data about Controller's workforce or candidate pipeline. Controller does not provide such data to Processor in the ordinary course of the Services.

## Annex C — Security Measures

Processor implements and maintains the following technical and organizational security measures:

### Encryption

- Personal Data is encrypted **in transit** using TLS 1.2 or higher
- Personal Data is encrypted **at rest** using AES-256 or equivalent industry-standard encryption

### Access Controls

- Role-based access controls limit Personal Data access to personnel with a legitimate need
- Multi-factor authentication is required for administrative access to systems processing Personal Data
- Access rights are reviewed periodically and revoked when no longer required

### Personnel Security

- Personnel with access to Personal Data are bound by written confidentiality obligations
- Personnel receive periodic training on data protection and security obligations

### Infrastructure Security

- Personal Data is stored on infrastructure provided by enterprise-grade cloud vendors with appropriate security certifications (e.g., SOC 2, ISO 27001)
- Environments are segregated between development, staging, and production

### Incident Response

- A documented security incident response process is maintained
- Security Incidents affecting Personal Data are notified per Section 5.2 of this DPA

### Data Minimization

- Only Personal Data necessary for the Services is collected
- Categories of Personal Data specifically excluded from Processing are set forth in Annex A

### Audit and Logging

- System access and administrative actions are logged for a reasonable retention period
- Logs are reviewed periodically for anomalous activity

> **Drafter's Note:** Security Annexes should be reviewed annually and updated as Antellion's infrastructure matures. When SOC 2 Type II is obtained, add a reference to the report and available attestation letter.

## Appendix: Notes for Counsel Review

Before using this template in live deals, a licensed attorney should review:

1. **Jurisdictional completeness.** Does the template adequately address the Data Protection Laws that apply to Antellion's customer base? If customers in EU/UK are expected within 12 months, confirm SCCs are correctly attached.
2. **Narrow-scope framing (Section 2.3).** Is the "narrow scope" language accurate and defensible? Should it be softened to avoid appearing to understate obligations?
3. **Audit rights (Section 9).** The "once per year" cap is vendor-friendly. Enterprise customers may push for more frequent audits. Decide acceptable negotiating range.
4. **International transfer mechanism (Section 7).** If EU/UK deals are expected, SCCs should be fully attached and Annex D created. If not, Section 7 can remain dormant.
5. **HIPAA / sector-specific law integration.** If Antellion plans to serve healthcare customers, a separate Business Associate Agreement (BAA) will be required. This DPA does not include BAA provisions.
6. **Annex B named vendor list.** Attorney should confirm the category-level disclosure in Annex B is sufficient or whether Antellion should proactively publish the named vendor list.
7. **Annex C security commitments.** Are the commitments in Annex C actually being met by current Antellion infrastructure? If not, either tighten infrastructure or soften commitments before first use.
8. **Liability alignment (Section 10).** Confirm that cross-referencing MSA Section 9 is sufficient, or whether DPA-specific liability language is required for certain jurisdictions.
9. **Relationship to customer-proposed DPAs.** Antellion's MSA Section 6.4 says it will execute the customer's DPA in substantially proposed form. Clarify when to use this template vs. accept the customer's, especially for strong-form DPAs from Fortune 500 customers.
10. **Language consistency with MSA.** Counsel should read MSA and DPA together to confirm terminology and obligations do not conflict.
