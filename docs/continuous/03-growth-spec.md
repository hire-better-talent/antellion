**How the first 1–2 deliveries are positioned in the engagement (proof-account framing):**

- **Internal label:** "proof account." Not "pilot" (implies discounted or tentative service) and not "cohort" (implies group dynamics that don't exist at n=1-2). Proof account = full-price, full-service Continuous engagement with explicit reference expectations baked in at signature.
- **Buyer-facing framing in the Forward Strategy Session:** the founder names the proof-account dynamic plainly — "you'd be among the first one or two Continuous engagements, which means the analyst's attention is concentrated, and in exchange we'd ask for a structured reference at the 90-day and 6-month marks." Honest, not apologetic. The founder-led model makes "concentrated analyst attention" a genuine benefit, not a cover for thin ops.
- **No price discount for proof-account status.** Discounting the retainer-grade product to acquire proof reference destroys the price anchor and signals desperation. The exchange is concentrated analyst attention + early-customer voice on roadmap, not money.
- **Engagement letter addendum** (lightweight, coo coordinates with legal): a single-paragraph proof-account clause naming reference expectations (structured testimonial at 90 days, longer-form quote at 6 months, willingness to take 1-2 prospect reference calls). Mutual escape: if the analyst judgment fails to be useful in the first 90 days, buyer is released from reference commitment.

**Testimonial format that captures judgment-vouching (vs product testimonials):**

Standard SaaS-style product testimonials ("dashboard saved us X hours") are wrong shape. Judgment-vouching testimonials need to capture:

- **The interpretive call that mattered.** Example structure (not copy): "The briefing told us what mattered and what not to overreact to — when X happened in [month], the analyst flagged it as drift but explicitly told us not to trigger the board comms playbook because Y. That call was right." The reference vouches for a *specific moment of senior judgment* rather than a generic outcome.
- **The named analyst by name.** With buyer permission, the reference names the founder explicitly. Anonymous "the Antellion analyst" reads weaker than named accountability.
- **A counterfactual.** What would have happened without the briefing — buyer makes the wrong call, overreacts, underreacts, misses the shift entirely. Counterfactual is what proves judgment was load-bearing, not nice-to-have.
- **Cadence of the engagement, not the platform.** Reference describes the quarterly readout conversation, the drift briefing rhythm, the escalation moment — not features, not dashboards, not data volumes.

Capture mechanism: a 30-minute structured interview at the 90-day mark conducted by growth (or founder), with a one-page testimonial draft returned to the buyer for edit and approval. Not a form, not a Trustpilot prompt. Reference quality is the artifact.

**How proof accounts are used in subsequent sales conversations:**

- **Forward Strategy Session referenceability.** Founder offers the proof-account reference call as part of the Forward Strategy close conversation for the third-onward Continuous prospect. Reference is offered to qualified prospects only (post-Diagnostic, in active Forward Strategy consideration), not as a marketing-page surface.

Default direction: no testimonials on the public page until at least 2 are in hand and the testimonial register has stabilized; gated reveal pattern (similar to founder bio) may apply.
- **Private sales packet.** Proof-account references appear in the private sales packet from sale 3 onward, alongside founder bio. This is the canonical surface for testimonial use until the public-page decision is made.
- **Forward Strategy Brief.** The Brief itself may cite a proof-account moment of judgment as a methodology illustration ("here is the kind of interpretive call this engagement is built to produce"), with reference permission. Stronger than abstract methodology language.

- **First-year price hold at renewal.** If a proof account renews year 2, they renew at the year-1 rate even if the published price has risen. Modest, partnership-shaped, contingent on renewal — gives the buyer a real partnership gesture only if they stay.
- **Structured reference expectations at 90 days and 6 months.** Reference obligations are an explicit clause in the proof-account engagement-letter addendum, not a vague "we'd love a testimonial" ask.
- **NO roadmap input as a formal benefit.** Creates founder calendar obligation without proportionate buyer value, and risks setting a precedent that compounds. Informal product feedback from proof accounts is welcome but not contractual.

Proof-account clause shape for coo's Addendum A drafting: full-price year 1 + 90-day and 6-month structured reference expectations + year-2 renewal price protected at year-1 rate if they renew.

## 1. Positioning

**Category:** *AI employer narrative surveillance.* Internal name; not for the website. Public-facing frame is **"executive intelligence service for AI employer narrative."**

It is **not** a monitoring tool, not a dashboard, not a SaaS platform. The buyer never logs in to use it. Output is a periodic intelligence product — drift briefings, severity-1 alerts, quarterly executive reads — delivered to inbox and read in a meeting.

**One-line frame:** *"Continuous keeps a standing watch on what AI says about you as an employer, so you find out before your CEO does."*

**Diagnostic vs Continuous in the buyer's mind:**

| | Diagnostic | Continuous |
|---|---|---|
| What it answers | "Where do we stand right now?" | "What changed, what's coming, what matters?" |
| Cadence | One-shot, 10 business days | Standing, year-round, recurring readouts |
| Deliverable shape | Report + readout call | Quarterly briefing + drift alerts + analyst channel |
| Buyer's mental category | Audit / engagement | Standing intelligence retainer |
| Decision pattern | Project budget | Strategic-functions retainer line (CHRO discretionary) |

Continuous is **not "another audit on a cadence."** Audits are point-in-time fact-establishment. Continuous is *drift detection across time* — only legible once you have a baseline and watch it move. This distinction is the entire moat (see §4).

## 2. Pricing

**Recommended band:** **$8,000–$11,000/month, 12-month minimum commit.** Land at **$9,500/month** as the default published-internal price; quote $11,000 to Strategic/Enterprise-scope buyers, $8,000 floor to mid-market.

This revises the launch-gated $6,500/month draft in `docs/offers/antellion-continuous.md` upward by ~45%. Reasoning:

- $6,500 reads as *tool spend.* $9,500 reads as *retainer.* The buyer's mental category determines the budget line; the budget line determines the renewal psychology. Tools get cut at the first cost review. Retainers get defended.
- The Diagnostic anchor is now established at $4,900 and Baseline at $14,000. Continuous at $9,500/month puts annual spend ($114K) just above Phase 2 Focused ($89K) — which positions it as the *natural next commitment* for buyers who completed Focused, not a downgrade.
- Symphony Talent's audit productization (May 2026) compresses the price ceiling on *point-in-time work.* It does not compress recurring-intelligence pricing because they have not entered the recurring layer. Underpricing Continuous to compete with a non-existent ST recurring product is a strategic error.
- The Hormozi 2x continuity ratio still works: standalone quarterly re-scan priced at ~$19K supports a $9,500 monthly subscription mathematically.

**Minimum commit:** 12 months. No month-to-month entry. The drift-detection value proposition is structurally invalid below 12 months — you cannot observe meaningful narrative drift in 90 days. Short-term subscribers experience the product as overpriced monitoring; only the 4-quarter buyer sees the actual deliverable.

**Activation fee:** $25,000, waived with annual commit (Hormozi Waived Fee). Real onboarding work — baseline calibration, alert tuning, customer-specific persona library setup.

**Relationship to Diagnostic / Phase 2:**

- **Diagnostic → Continuous direct:** not the default upgrade path. Diagnostic is a $4,900 P-card price point; Continuous is a $114K committee budget. Too large a jump.
- **Baseline → Continuous:** possible but not pushed. Baseline is mandatory Phase 1; Continuous after Baseline-only is a path we support if asked but do not lead with.
- **Phase 2 → Continuous (canonical path):** Phase 2 close is the moment of peak trust and the natural Continuous attachment point. Same signature page. First quarterly re-scan included = ~$19K continuity discount.
- **No bundled price for Phase 2 + Continuous.** Stays separate line items so the recurring revenue is visible and the Continuous renewal stands on its own.

Target attach rate: 50% of Phase 2 closes (revised down from 60% in original draft — higher list price warrants conservative attach assumption).

## 3. ICP fit — who buys Continuous after the Diagnostic

**Buyer:** Same CHRO / CPO / CMO buyer as Diagnostic, but a *subset* of that population. Specifically the ones whose Diagnostic delivery surfaced one or more of:

1. **Findings indicating active narrative drift** — AI's story about the company changed materially in the last 6-12 months (layoff coverage, leadership turnover, M&A, public controversy). These buyers feel the velocity of the problem and Continuous sells itself.
2. **Findings indicating competitive movement** — a named talent-competitor is closing the citation gap or gaining narrative ground. Buyer wants to know when it shifts again.
3. **Findings indicating high-volatility candidate journey stages** — Consideration or Evaluation stages where AI's answer varies meaningfully across models or runs. These are the buyers whose narrative is *unstable*, and unstable narratives need ongoing watch.
4. **Buyer demonstrates board-defensibility anxiety** — phrases like "I need to be able to answer if my CEO asks," "the board is asking about AI," "I don't want to be caught flat-footed."

**Signal from Diagnostic delivery that flags "needs Continuous":**

The Diagnostic findings call surfaces three classes of buyer reaction. Map each to next-step routing:

| Reaction class | Continuous fit |
|---|---|
| "This is a one-time problem to fix and move on" | Low — route to Phase 2 Focused, not Continuous |
| "This is a system we need to watch" | **High — primary Continuous candidate** |
| "Interesting but not urgent right now" | Low — route to next year's Diagnostic |

The class-2 reaction is the qualification signal. The analyst running the Diagnostic readout should be listening for it.

**Anti-ICP (do not push Continuous to):**

- Buyers below 2,000 employees (insufficient narrative surface area to produce meaningful monthly alerts)
- Buyers whose Diagnostic surfaced <8 findings (signal that the narrative is currently stable; Continuous would underdeliver against the fee)
- Buyers whose primary stakeholder was a CMO with no CHRO partnership (continuity-budget access is harder to renew without the CHRO economic owner)

## 4. Defensibility vs Symphony Talent

**The expected objection:** *"Isn't this just an audit on a cadence? Why can't [Symphony Talent / agency / our own employer brand team] do this quarterly?"*

**The "photograph vs. time-lapse" bridge — primary buyer-language frame (added 2026-05-18 with Authority Surface Map). Compliance-sentry pass complete 2026-05-18: PASS with one redline applied to line 221 (sector-narrowing fix). Bridge paragraph + Forward Strategy Session script (§5 a.1) cleared as canonical for coo's Forward Strategy Session procedure spec.**

The Authority Surface Map is the asset that gives this distinction its strongest articulation. The Diagnostic produces a depth read across the twelve AI-cited surface categories — Anchored, Present, Thin, or Absent — at a single point in time. That photograph is durable and decision-grade for the next quarter. The question it doesn't answer is: *which of those depths is moving, in which direction, and at what rate?* A single Authority Deficit on a Specialist surface could be a thirty-six-month structural condition or a six-month deterioration that started when a competitor began publishing into the same surface. Same finding, different remediation urgency. Continuous is the engagement that produces the second view: the tracked curve of authority depth per surface, the named analyst's read on what's moving, and the drift briefing that says *this surface has dropped from Present to Thin in the last two quarters and here is why*. Photograph vs. time-lapse — same subject, different epistemic question.

**The answer (in buyer language):**

> An audit is a snapshot. Continuous is a baseline plus drift detection plus a standing analyst relationship. The deliverable that matters most is the one you only get when you have a year of baseline behind it — the moment we tell you, with evidence, that authority depth on the citation surfaces AI is reading about your `{narrative-area}` moved from Anchored to Present last quarter and here is which surface dropped and why. You cannot produce that from a single audit, no matter how good. You produce it from accumulated longitudinal observation of authority depth across the AI-cited candidate-intent surfaces in the scored corpus, calibrated alert thresholds, and an analyst who knows your narrative well enough to recognize when it shifts. That is what Continuous is.

**On the `{narrative-area}` slot (compliance-sentry redline, 2026-05-18):** the founder fills this slot live from the buyer's actual Diagnostic findings — the narrative area where the buyer's strongest authority sits or where drift would be most consequential. Sector-rotate by buyer: "sales leadership pipeline" for a revenue-org-led discussion, "customer success team culture" for a CS-org-led one, "industrial engineering reputation" for manufacturing, "commercial talent narrative" for cross-functional. Never hard-code "engineering culture" — RULE-10 sector-narrowing in canonical buyer-speech.

**Why this defense holds structurally, not just rhetorically:**

1. **Longitudinal data compounds.** Antellion's value to a Continuous customer is higher at month 12 than at month 1. ST's audit product produces equal value every time it runs. A competitor entering the recurring market starts from zero baseline for every account.
2. **Calibration is customer-specific.** Continuous alert thresholds are tuned to the customer's narrative shape — what counts as "drift" for a 50K-headcount industrial manufacturer differs from a 5K-headcount fintech. This calibration is proprietary per-account and not transferable.
3. **The relationship is the moat.** The named analyst, the Slack channel, the meeting cadence — the things buyers do not want to re-stand-up with a new vendor every quarter. Audit-on-cadence has none of these.
4. **Operating model is structurally different from ST.** ST's audit productization (May 2026) scales by adding analysts to a service team. Continuous scales by giving one named analyst leverage through agent-assisted production. From the buyer's perspective: ST means "your account passes through a team of unnamed associates"; Continuous means "the same named analyst owns your narrative for the term of the engagement, and their time is spent on interpretation rather than data assembly." This is not feature differentiation; it is delivery-model differentiation, which is harder to copy because copying it requires ST to rebuild their service economics.
5. **ST's distribution advantage does not apply here.** ST sells to employer brand teams. Continuous sells to CHRO/CPO with CMO co-sponsorship — adjacent buying centers, different procurement paths.

**What we do NOT say:** "We do continuous monitoring, they only do audits." That's a feature comparison. The defensibility narrative is structural: *cadence + calibration + relationship + operating model* compounds into something an audit-on-cadence cannot replicate, regardless of how often the audit runs.

**What we also do NOT say:** "Our AI agent watches your AI visibility." This collapses the category back into dashboard/monitoring-tool framing and erases the founder-analyst accountability that justifies the retainer fee. The agent is an internal production tool; it does not have a buyer-facing identity. Compliance-sentry will flag any drift into this framing.

## 5. Upgrade-path mechanics — how the Diagnostic sells Continuous

The Diagnostic delivery is itself the primary Continuous sales motion. The mechanics below build the natural upgrade.

**(a) Drift Watch as required Diagnostic section — the methodological on-ramp.**

Drift Watch is now a mandatory section in every Diagnostic report (ratified May 13). Final 1-2 pages identify 3-5 specific aspects of the buyer's AI narrative likely to shift in the next 6-12 months and what would trigger each shift. (Example direction: a forward-looking analyst note tying a competitor's announced future move to a predictable softening of one of the buyer's narrative claims within a defined window.)

**How growth talks about Drift Watch pre-purchase** (cold email, /diagnostic page, sales conversation):

- **Frame as methodology, not as a Continuous teaser.** Drift Watch is what a baseline becomes useful *for* — without forward analysis, a snapshot is just an inventory. Buyer should read about Drift Watch and think "this is a more serious diagnostic," not "this is a free preview of the subscription."
- **Do NOT call it a "Continuous preview" or "sample of what Continuous would do."** That cheapens both products: makes Diagnostic feel like a sales funnel and makes Continuous feel like the paid version of something they already got.
- **Do NOT promise specific drift findings in pre-purchase materials.** Drift Watch is real analyst forecasting; the specific forecasts are produced from the buyer's actual scan data and cannot be previewed honestly. Pre-purchase framing should describe the section's existence and methodological role, not its contents.
- **Allowed framing direction:** "Every Diagnostic includes a Drift Watch section identifying where your AI narrative is most likely to shift in the next 6-12 months and what would trigger it. This is the part of the report that converts a snapshot into a starting point." Conveys methodology, sets up Continuous, makes no promises that depend on findings.

The conceptual bridge: Diagnostic baselines the current state → Drift Watch identifies what could move → Phase 2 fixes the current problems → Continuous watches the moving surface afterward. Each step earns the next.

**(a.1) Forward Strategy Session script — moving from Diagnostic findings into the Continuous renewal pitch using the Authority Surface Map (added 2026-05-18).**

The Forward Strategy Session is the moment the founder pivots from "here is what your Diagnostic found" to "here is the engagement that would produce continued reads on what's moving." The Authority Surface Map gives this pivot a clean handhold via the authority-depth-as-tracked-curve narrative. Suggested four-sentence sequence the founder uses verbatim or in close paraphrase, immediately after walking through the Authority Surface Map section of the Diagnostic with the buyer:

> So that's the depth read across the twelve surfaces as of the corpus we scored — three categories Anchored, four Present, five Thin or Absent, and the Authority Deficit findings naming the surfaces where deliberate investment would move the score. The question that read can't answer on its own is whether any of those depths is actively moving, in which direction, and at what rate — because depth on a Specialist surface that's been Thin for three years is a different remediation conversation than depth that dropped from Present to Thin last quarter because a competitor started publishing into the same surface. What we'd do if you continued the engagement is keep a tracked curve on each of the twelve surfaces, surface drift as it shows up, and bring you a quarterly read on what moved and what didn't — same authority-depth measurement methodology, observed over time instead of at a moment. That's what Continuous is built to do, and the natural attachment point is the next-step engagement we're discussing today.

**Why this language works:**

- Re-uses the canonical phrase implicitly through the Authority Surface Map vocabulary already established in the section walkthrough
- Builds the Continuous case from the buyer's own Diagnostic findings, not from a feature list
- Names the analytical question Continuous answers — drift rate and direction — that the Diagnostic structurally cannot
- Avoids "monitor," "watch," "dashboard," and AI-agent framing
- Lands the close on attachment, not on a separate decision moment (consistent with §5 (c) single-signature mechanic)

**Compliance lock:** every clause passes RULE-01 (no comparative-without-replacement), RULE-02 (canonical phrase, no web-wide claim), no scan-runner framing. **Compliance-sentry pass complete 2026-05-18 — this block cleared as written for canonical use in coo's Forward Strategy Session procedure spec.**

**(b) Findings-call CTA — buyer-led, not vendor-led.**

The Diagnostic readout call ends not with a Phase 2 pitch but with a question to the buyer about what they want to happen when something in the Drift Watch shifts. The answer is almost always some version of "I want to be told before I find out from someone else." That answer is the Continuous pitch in the buyer's own language, which is the version that survives the procurement conversation.

**(c) Phase 2-attached close on a single signature page (canonical path).**

The Phase 2 close is the canonical Continuous attachment moment. Both engagements are structured on a single signature page. High-level only — the coo Phase 2 close procedure spec owns the operational checklist; this section owns what the page must structure so buyer comprehension is correct.

**What the single signature page must structure:**

| Section | What it commits to | What's contingent |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 2 scope | Fixed-scope remediation track (Focused / Strategic / Enterprise) priced as a separate line item | Begins on countersignature; deliverables scoped from Baseline findings |
| Continuous engagement | $9,500/mo × 12 months; named founder analyst; quarterly executive readout cadence; drift briefing cadence; analyst channel | Activation begins after Phase 2 kickoff; first quarterly re-scan included as continuity discount |

Does NOT apply to: every line of data assembly, every alert generation step, raw scan execution — those are agent-assisted production work, and the engagement letter says so |
| Cadence commitments | Quarterly readout (founder-led), monthly drift briefings (founder-signed), severity-1 alerts within the SLA defined by coo spec | Severity-1 SLA depends on architect spec resolution; reference cleanly |

If buyer signs Baseline-only or Diagnostic-only, Continuous is offered with a 60-day window at the same continuity discount. After 60 days, standard pricing applies. Framed as fairness ("we hold the discount because you are still in the analyst's active queue") rather than urgency.

**What we do NOT do:** auto-renewal traps, free-trial Continuous, "first month free," dashboard demos. All read as desperate to enterprise buyers and damage the retainer-grade positioning.

## 5b. Engagement-letter framing — what the founder commits to in writing

The engagement letter is the legally meaningful artifact behind the signature page. It must reflect "Founder-led intelligence service, agent-assisted production" honestly. Growth owns the framing; legal owns the language.

**What the engagement letter commits the founder to** (in writing, signed):

- Founder is the named analyst of record for the engagement term
- Founder signs every quarterly briefing and every monthly drift briefing
- Founder personally conducts the quarterly executive readout call
- Founder is the named point of escalation for severity-1 drift alerts and any interpretation dispute
- Founder reviews and approves every artifact before it leaves the engagement

**What the engagement letter explicitly does NOT promise:**

- That every line of every artifact is hand-written by the founder
- That data assembly, movement-tracker drafting, watch-list maintenance, or query refresh is performed manually by the founder
- That the founder is the sole producer of any deliverable
- A specific number of findings, alerts, or drift signals per period
- Real-time monitoring or 24/7 availability outside defined SLAs

**Language guardrail:** the engagement letter describes the founder's role as "named analyst, accountable for interpretation and sign-off." It describes production tooling as "agent-assisted production methods used in service of the analyst's work." It does NOT describe an "AI agent" as the buyer's counterparty, deliverable producer, or service interface. Compliance-sentry reviews before circulation.

**Why this matters legally:** overclaiming founder-direct involvement on every artifact creates misrepresentation risk if a buyer audits delivery process. Overclaiming agent autonomy creates the opposite problem (buyer feels they bought a bot). Honest framing closes both gaps.

**Why this matters reputationally:** the buyer is a CHRO/CPO who will defend this line item to their CEO and board. They need a defense that survives a question like "is a human actually looking at this?" The honest answer — "yes, the named analyst signs every briefing and runs every quarterly readout; their time is spent on interpretation rather than data assembly" — is the defense. The dishonest answer ("yes, the founder personally produces every artifact") collapses under any scrutiny of operational scale.

## 5c. The 6-12 month milestone narrative — answering "will Jordan still be signing my briefing in two years?"

The operating model has a built-in milestone: after 3-5 Continuous clients or 6-12 months from first sale (whichever first), the founder revisits the operating mix. Sophisticated buyers will ask the corresponding question during the Phase 2 close conversation or during renewal: *"Is this a founder-led service forever, or does it scale into something else?"*

**The honest narrative direction** (paraphrased, not copy):

> Yes, the founder remains accountable. "Founder-led" describes who signs the briefing, who runs the quarterly readout, and who interprets the findings — that's the constant. "Agent-assisted production" describes how the underlying data assembly is done — and that mix evolves as the service scales. Today the founder spends most of their analyst time on interpretation rather than data preparation, which is exactly why the retainer buys senior judgment rather than headcount hours. As the practice grows, that mix may shift further toward agent-assisted production for the data layer and may eventually include a small research-ops function — but the named-analyst accountability for your engagement is the contract, and it doesn't change without your knowledge.

**Why this answer holds:**

- It tells the truth about the operating model rather than hiding it
- It explains *why* the founder can sustain the relationship at scale (leverage, not heroics)
- It frames future evolution as transparent and contract-bound, not as a downgrade
- It matches the engagement letter, so a buyer who later reads the legal document does not feel misled

**What growth must NOT say:**

- "It will always be just me." Untrue at scale, and untrue is the whole problem.
- "We have a team of analysts." Untrue today, and the wrong category signal (collapses us into ST's service-team model).
- "Eventually an AI will do it." Worse than untrue — it cedes the entire defensibility narrative.

**Operational tie-in:** at the 3-5 client / 6-12 month milestone, the founder makes one of three calls (per `project_continuous_operating_model_may2026`): hire fractional research ops, keep founder-review-only, or promote agent-assisted delivery further. Whichever call is made, growth must refresh this narrative section and the engagement-letter framing in lockstep. The "named analyst stays accountable" line is the durable promise; everything else is allowed to evolve.
